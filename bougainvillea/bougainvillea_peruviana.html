Bougainvillea peruviana – Peruvian Bougainvillea

Bougainvillea peruviana is a less common species known for its slender growth habit and vivid magenta bracts, contributing to the diversity of bougainvillea forms.

Description

A lighter, more open-growing vine compared to other species, with narrower leaves and thinner stems. It produces bright magenta to reddish-purple bracts that are smaller but abundant.

Etymology

The name “peruviana” reflects its origin in Peru, where it naturally occurs.

Taxonomy

Family: Nyctaginaceae
Genus: Bougainvillea
Species: B. peruviana

Distribution and Habitat

Native to Peru, typically found in warm, dry regions and adapted to sunny, open environments.

Ecology

Supports pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its lighter structure allows it to blend into mixed plant communities.

Cultivation

  • Full sun preferred
  • Well-drained soil
  • Low water requirements once established
  • Often used in hybridization due to its unique traits

Cultivars

  • Rare in pure form; more commonly used in hybrid bougainvillea varieties

Symbolism

Represents natural beauty, subtle elegance, and adaptability in harsher climates.
