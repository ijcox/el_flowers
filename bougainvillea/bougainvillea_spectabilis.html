Bougainvillea spectabilis – Great Bougainvillea

Bougainvillea spectabilis is one of the most well-known species of bougainvillea, admired for its large, vivid bracts and vigorous climbing growth.

Description

A robust, woody vine with thick stems and noticeable thorns. It produces large clusters of bright magenta to purple bracts surrounding small white flowers, creating a striking display.

Etymology

The species name “spectabilis” means “showy” or “spectacular,” referring to its bold and eye-catching floral display.

Taxonomy

Family: Nyctaginaceae
Genus: Bougainvillea
Species: B. spectabilis

Distribution and Habitat

Native to Brazil, commonly found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in warm climates with plenty of sunlight.

Ecology

Attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its dense growth also provides shelter for small animals and birds.

Cultivation

  • Full sun for best flowering
  • Well-drained soil
  • Drought tolerant once established
  • Requires support for climbing

Cultivars

  • Typically grown in its natural form, though some hybrids include this species

Symbolism

Represents passion, beauty, and resilience, often associated with tropical vibrancy and warmth.
