Bougainvillea glabra – Paper Flower

Bougainvillea glabra is a popular ornamental species known as the “paper flower” due to its thin, papery bracts and elegant appearance.

Description

A fast-growing, climbing shrub with smoother stems and fewer thorns than other species. It produces abundant, delicate bracts in shades of purple, pink, and white, giving it a lighter, more refined look.

Etymology

The name “glabra” means “smooth” or “hairless,” referring to its relatively smooth leaves and stems.

Taxonomy

Family: Nyctaginaceae
Genus: Bougainvillea
Species: B. glabra

Distribution and Habitat

Native to Brazil, widely cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions around the world, especially in gardens and urban landscapes.

Ecology

Provides nectar for pollinators such as butterflies and bees. Its climbing habit can also offer shelter for small wildlife.

Cultivation

  • Full sun exposure
  • Well-drained soil
  • Moderate watering; avoid overwatering
  • Ideal for trellises, walls, and containers

Cultivars

  • Commonly used in hybrid breeding programs
  • Numerous ornamental varieties with different colors

Symbolism

Symbolizes beauty, creativity, and adaptability, often associated with warm climates and decorative landscapes.
