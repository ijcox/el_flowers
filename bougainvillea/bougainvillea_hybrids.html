Bougainvillea Hybrids – Cultivated Garden Varieties

Bougainvillea hybrids are cultivated varieties developed by crossing different species to enhance color, size, growth habit, and adaptability for ornamental use.

Description

Hybrids vary widely in form, ranging from compact shrubs to vigorous climbers. They are known for their exceptionally bright and diverse bract colors, including pink, red, orange, yellow, white, and even multicolored varieties.

Etymology

The term “hybrid” refers to plants bred from two or more parent species, combining desirable characteristics from each.

Taxonomy

Family: Nyctaginaceae
Genus: Bougainvillea
Group: Hybrid cultivars (often derived from B. spectabilis, B. glabra, and B. peruviana)

Distribution and Habitat

Grown worldwide in tropical, subtropical, and warm temperate regions, especially in gardens, landscapes, and urban settings.

Ecology

Provide nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Their dense growth can also offer shelter for small wildlife.

Cultivation

  • Full sun for maximum flowering
  • Well-drained soil
  • Moderate watering; avoid overwatering
  • Can be trained as climbers, shrubs, or bonsai

Cultivars

  • ‘Barbara Karst’ – bright red bracts
  • ‘California Gold’ – golden-yellow bracts
  • ‘Raspberry Ice’ – variegated leaves with pink bracts
  • ‘Singapore White’ – elegant white bracts

Symbolism

Symbolizes vibrancy, creativity, and adaptability, reflecting the wide diversity and beauty achieved through cultivation.
