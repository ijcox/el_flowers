Snapdragon

Snapdragons are popular garden flowers known for their unique dragon-shaped blooms that can be gently squeezed open.

Description

Snapdragon flowers grow on tall spikes and come in many colors including pink, red, yellow, white, purple, and orange. Each bloom resembles a dragon’s mouth that opens when squeezed.

They are widely used in ornamental beds and cut flower arrangements.

Taxonomy

Snapdragons belong to the genus Antirrhinum in the family Plantaginaceae. They are native to the Mediterranean region.

Species

Antirrhinum Majus – Common Snapdragon
Antirrhinum Barrelieri – Mediterranean Snapdragon
Antirrhinum Molle – Soft Snapdragon

Etymology

The name Antirrhinum comes from Greek meaning “like a nose,” referring to the flower’s shape.

Ecology

Snapdragons attract bees, especially large bees that can open the flower’s “mouth.”

Diseases and pests

  • Aphids on flower spikes
  • Rust fungus on leaves
  • Powdery mildew in humid conditions
  • Spider mites in dry heat

Cultivation

Snapdragons are widely grown as cool-season ornamentals for garden color and cut flowers.

  • Light: Full sun
  • Soil: Well-drained, fertile soil
  • Water: Moderate
  • Planting: Seeds or transplants
  • Maintenance: Deadhead for longer blooming

Snapdragon in culture

Snapdragons symbolize strength, grace, and protection.
