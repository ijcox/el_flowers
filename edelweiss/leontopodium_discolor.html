Leontopodium discolor – Two-toned Edelweiss

Edelweiss discolor is a rare alpine species noted for subtle variation in its bract coloration, often appearing two-toned in certain populations.

Description

A compact alpine perennial with woolly bracts that may show mixed white and slightly darker tones, forming a star-like flower head.

Etymology

“Discolor” means “of different colors,” referring to its variable bract coloration.

Taxonomy

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Leontopodium
Species: L. discolor

Distribution and Habitat

Found in scattered alpine regions of Asia, typically in rocky, high-elevation habitats.

Ecology

Adapted to extreme alpine conditions with strong UV exposure, cold temperatures, and thin soils.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Excellent drainage required
  • Rock garden or alpine trough preferred
  • Very low nutrient soil

Cultivars

  • Rare in cultivation; mostly preserved in wild collections

Symbolism

Represents uniqueness, rarity, and natural variation in alpine ecosystems.
