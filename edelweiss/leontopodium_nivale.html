Leontopodium nivale – Snow Edelweiss

Edelweiss nivale is a high-altitude alpine species adapted to extreme cold, wind, and snow exposure.

Description

A compact perennial with dense woolly white bracts forming star-shaped flower heads, giving a snow-covered appearance.

Etymology

“Nivale” means “snowy,” referencing its snowy white, felted appearance and alpine snow habitats.

Taxonomy

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Leontopodium
Species: L. nivale

Distribution and Habitat

Found in high alpine regions of Europe and adjacent mountain systems, often above the tree line.

Ecology

Specialized for short growing seasons, low nutrients, and intense UV radiation.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Rocky, well-drained soil
  • Low water requirements
  • Requires alpine or rock garden conditions

Cultivars

  • Rarely cultivated; mostly wild form maintained

Symbolism

Represents purity, resilience, and untouched nature.
