Leontopodium alpinum – Common Edelweiss

Edelweiss alpinum is a small alpine plant famous for its star-shaped white woolly flowers growing in harsh mountain environments.

Description

A low-growing perennial producing white, felted bracts forming a star-like flower head adapted to cold, high-altitude conditions.

Etymology

“Leontopodium” means “lion’s paw,” referring to the fuzzy flower shape, and “alpinum” indicates its alpine habitat.

Taxonomy

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Leontopodium
Species: L. alpinum

Distribution and Habitat

Native to the mountain ranges of Europe, especially the Alps, typically found on rocky limestone slopes.

Ecology

Adapted to extreme cold, wind, and UV exposure; pollinated by insects in short alpine summers.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Very well-drained, rocky soil
  • Low nutrient requirements
  • Cold hardy alpine plant

Cultivars

  • Selected compact garden forms exist but remain close to wild type

Symbolism

Represents courage, rugged beauty, and enduring love.
