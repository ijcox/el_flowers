Leontopodium soulei – Asian Edelweiss

Edelweiss soulei is an Asian alpine species found in mountainous regions with cool, high-altitude climates.

Description

A compact perennial with woolly white bracts forming star-like flower heads adapted to harsh alpine conditions.

Etymology

Named in honor of botanist Soulié, who studied Asian flora.

Taxonomy

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Leontopodium
Species: L. soulei

Distribution and Habitat

Native to parts of China and surrounding mountain ranges, typically found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Ecology

Adapted to cold, UV-intense environments with short growing seasons and poor soils.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Very well-drained soil
  • Alpine or rock garden conditions
  • Low humidity preferred

Cultivars

  • Primarily wild forms in cultivation

Symbolism

Represents rarity, resilience, and remote natural beauty.
