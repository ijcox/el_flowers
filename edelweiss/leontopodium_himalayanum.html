Leontopodium himalayanum – Himalayan Edelweiss

Edelweiss himalayanum is a high-altitude species adapted to the extreme conditions of the Himalayan mountain range.

Description

A hardy alpine perennial with woolly white bracts forming compact star-shaped flower heads suited to cold, windy environments.

Etymology

“Himalayanum” indicates its native range in the Himalayas.

Taxonomy

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Leontopodium
Species: L. himalayanum

Distribution and Habitat

Native to the Himalayas, found in rocky slopes, alpine meadows, and high mountain passes.

Ecology

Survives in thin soils with intense sunlight, cold temperatures, and short growing seasons.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Excellent drainage required
  • Rock garden or alpine trough conditions
  • Very cold hardy but sensitive to humidity

Cultivars

  • Mostly wild-type in cultivation

Symbolism

Represents endurance, isolation beauty, and high-mountain purity.
