Gaillardia

Gaillardia, commonly known as blanket flower, is a bright and hardy flowering plant recognized for its red, orange, and yellow daisy-like blooms.

Description

Gaillardia produces long-lasting flowers with vivid, flame-like coloration. It is drought tolerant and thrives in hot, sunny environments.

The plant is widely used in wildflower gardens and low-maintenance landscapes.

Taxonomy

Gaillardia belongs to the genus Gaillardia in the family Asteraceae. It is native to North and South America.

Species

Gaillardia Pulchella – Firewheel
Gaillardia Aristata – Perennial Blanket Flower
Gaillardia Aestivalis – Prairie Gaillardia
Gaillardia Fimbriata – Fringed Blanket Flower
Gaillardia Hybrid – Garden Hybrid

Etymology

The genus Gaillardia is named after French magistrate and botanist M. Gaillard de Charentonneau.

Ecology

Gaillardia is an important nectar source for bees and butterflies in dry grassland ecosystems.

Diseases and pests

  • Powdery mildew in humid climates
  • Aphids on flower buds
  • Root rot in poorly drained soil
  • Leaf spot fungal infections

Cultivation

Gaillardia is widely cultivated for its resilience, long bloom time, and ability to thrive in poor soils.

  • Light: Full sun
  • Soil: Well-drained, sandy soil
  • Water: Low; drought tolerant
  • Planting: Seeds or transplants in spring
  • Maintenance: Deadhead to prolong flowering

Gaillardia in culture

Gaillardia symbolizes cheerfulness, endurance, and natural warmth due to its bright flame-like colors.
