Carnations

Carnations are classic ornamental flowers known for their ruffled petals, long-lasting blooms, and sweet fragrance. They are among the most widely cultivated cut flowers in the world.

Description

Carnations are herbaceous perennials with slender stems and narrow, bluish-green leaves. Flowers are typically double or semi-double with serrated petal edges.

Colors include white, pink, red, yellow, and variegated patterns. They are especially valued in floral arrangements due to their long vase life.

Taxonomy

Carnations belong to the genus Dianthus in the family Caryophyllaceae. The genus also includes pinks and sweet williams.

Species

Notable carnation-related species include:

Dianthus Caryophyllus – Carnation
Dianthus Barbatus – Sweet William
Dianthus Plumarius – Cottage pink
Dianthus Deltoides – Maiden pink
Dianthus Chinensis – China pink

Etymology

The genus name Dianthus comes from Greek meaning “divine flower.” The word “carnation” is believed to come from “coronation” or the Latin “carnis” meaning flesh, referring to the original pinkish flower color.

Ecology

Carnations are not typically major wildflowers but cultivated forms may attract bees and butterflies. Wild Dianthus species grow in Mediterranean and temperate rocky habitats.

They prefer dry, well-drained soils and open sunny environments.

Diseases and pests

  • Aphids may attack buds and stems.
  • Thrips can damage flower petals.
  • Fungal leaf spots may occur in humid conditions.
  • Rust diseases occasionally affect foliage.
  • Root rot develops in poorly drained soil.

Cultivation

Carnations are widely grown for cut flowers, garden borders, and greenhouse production. They are favored for their durability and fragrance.

  • Light: Full sun preferred.
  • Soil: Well-drained, slightly alkaline soil.
  • Water: Moderate; avoid overwatering.
  • Maintenance: Deadhead for continuous blooming.
  • Propagation: Cuttings or seed.

Carnations in culture

Carnations symbolize love, admiration, distinction, and remembrance. They are widely used in ceremonies, celebrations, and memorial arrangements.

Different colors carry different meanings in floral traditions across cultures.
