Oenothera fruticosa – Narrowleaf Evening Primrose

Evening Primrose (Evening Primrose) fruticosa is a hardy perennial known for its bright yellow flowers and upright form.

Description

A clump-forming perennial with narrow leaves and clusters of bright yellow, four-petaled flowers that bloom in summer.

Etymology

“Fruticosa” means “shrubby,” referring to its bush-like growth habit.

Taxonomy

Family: Onagraceae
Genus: Oenothera
Species: O. fruticosa

Distribution and Habitat

Native to eastern North America, found in dry woods, meadows, and rocky slopes.

Ecology

Provides nectar for bees and other pollinators during summer flowering.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Dry, well-drained soil
  • Drought tolerant perennial
  • Low maintenance garden plant

Cultivars

  • ‘Fyrverkeri’ – compact bright yellow form

Symbolism

Represents endurance, brightness, and resilience.
