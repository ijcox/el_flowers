Oenothera drummondii – Beach Evening Primrose

Evening Primrose (Evening Primrose) drummondii is a coastal species adapted to sandy shorelines and salt exposure.

Description

A low-growing perennial producing bright yellow flowers that open in the evening, often forming mats along coastal dunes.

Etymology

Named after Thomas Drummond, a Scottish botanist and plant collector.

Taxonomy

Family: Onagraceae
Genus: Oenothera
Species: O. drummondii

Distribution and Habitat

Native to coastal regions of the southeastern United States and parts of the Gulf of Mexico, thriving in sandy beaches and dunes.

Ecology

Adapted to salt spray, wind, and shifting sands; important stabilizer of dune ecosystems.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Sandy, well-drained soil
  • Salt tolerant
  • Ideal for coastal gardens

Cultivars

  • Mainly wild-type in cultivation

Symbolism

Represents resilience, adaptability, and coastal strength.
