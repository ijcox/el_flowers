Oenothera biennis – Common Evening Primrose

Evening Primrose (Evening Primrose) biennis is a biennial wildflower known for its bright yellow flowers that open in the evening.

Description

A tall biennial plant forming a rosette in its first year and producing yellow, four-petaled flowers on tall spikes in the second year.

Etymology

“Oenothera” is derived from Greek, traditionally linked to “wine seeker,” though its exact origin is debated. “Biennis” refers to its two-year life cycle.

Taxonomy

Family: Onagraceae
Genus: Oenothera
Species: O. biennis

Distribution and Habitat

Native to North America, now naturalized in Europe and other temperate regions, often found in roadsides and disturbed soils.

Ecology

Important nectar source for nocturnal moths, especially hawk moths.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Dry to moderately moist soil
  • Self-seeding biennial
  • Low maintenance wildflower

Cultivars

  • Mostly wild-type grown for naturalized gardens

Symbolism

Represents renewal, evening calm, and resilience.
