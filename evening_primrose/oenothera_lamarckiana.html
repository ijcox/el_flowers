Oenothera lamarckiana – Large-flowered Evening Primrose

Evening Primrose (Evening Primrose) lamarckiana is a robust species known for its tall growth and large yellow blooms.

Description

A vigorous biennial or short-lived perennial producing large yellow flowers on tall stems, often exceeding 1.5 meters in height.

Etymology

Named in honor of Jean-Baptiste Lamarck, the French naturalist.

Taxonomy

Family: Onagraceae
Genus: Oenothera
Species: O. lamarckiana

Distribution and Habitat

Historically associated with North America, now widely naturalized in Europe in disturbed soils and open fields.

Ecology

Provides nectar for nocturnal pollinators such as moths and supports generalist insects.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Self-seeding in open habitats
  • Fast-growing and tall species

Cultivars

  • Primarily wild form in cultivation

Symbolism

Represents transformation, scientific discovery, and natural variation.
