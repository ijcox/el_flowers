Oenothera speciosa – Pink Evening Primrose

Evening Primrose (Evening Primrose) speciosa is a spreading perennial known for its delicate pink flowers.

Description

A low-growing perennial producing soft pink to white flowers that open in the evening and fade to deeper tones with age.

Etymology

“Speciosa” means “showy,” referring to its attractive blooms.

Taxonomy

Family: Onagraceae
Genus: Oenothera
Species: O. speciosa

Distribution and Habitat

Native to the southern United States and Mexico, often found in prairies and open grasslands.

Ecology

Attracts bees and nocturnal moths and spreads readily in suitable habitats.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Dry to moderately moist soil
  • Spreading groundcover habit
  • Drought tolerant once established

Cultivars

  • ‘Siskiyou’ – deep pink selection

Symbolism

Represents gentle beauty, renewal, and natural abundance.
