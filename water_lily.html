Water Lily

Water lilies are aquatic flowering plants known for their floating leaves and elegant blooms on still water surfaces.

Description

Water lily flowers float or rise just above the water surface and come in white, pink, yellow, blue, and purple varieties. Large round leaves float on water, providing shade and habitat.

They are commonly found in ponds, lakes, and slow-moving waters.

Taxonomy

Water lilies belong mainly to the family Nymphaeaceae.

Species

Nymphaea Alba – European White Water Lily
Nymphaea Tetragona – Dwarf Water Lily
Nymphaea Caerulea – Blue Water Lily

Etymology

The name Nymphaea comes from Greek mythology, referring to water nymphs.

Ecology

Water lilies provide shade, oxygenation, and habitat for fish and aquatic insects.

Diseases and pests

  • Aphids on leaves
  • Water lily beetles
  • Fungal leaf spot
  • Root rot in poor water conditions

Cultivation

Water lilies are widely grown in ornamental ponds and water gardens.

  • Light: Full sun
  • Water: Still or slow-moving water
  • Depth: Shallow to moderate pond depth
  • Planting: Rhizomes in aquatic soil
  • Maintenance: Remove dead leaves regularly

Water Lily in culture

Water lilies symbolize purity, enlightenment, and rebirth in many cultures.
