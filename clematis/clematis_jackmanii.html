Clematis ‘Jackmanii’ – Hybrid Clematis

Clematis ‘Jackmanii’ is one of the most famous hybrid clematis varieties, widely grown for its rich purple flowers and vigorous climbing habit.

Description

A deciduous climbing vine producing large, velvety, deep purple flowers with contrasting yellow stamens during summer.

Etymology

Named after George Jackman & Son nursery, which introduced this hybrid in the 19th century.

Taxonomy

Family: Ranunculaceae
Genus: Clematis
Cultivar: C. ‘Jackmanii’

Distribution and Habitat

A cultivated hybrid, grown widely in temperate gardens around the world.

Ecology

Attracts bees and other pollinators during its long flowering period.

Cultivation

  • Full sun for flowers, shaded roots preferred
  • Well-drained, fertile soil
  • Requires support structures for climbing
  • Prune in late winter or early spring

Cultivars

  • ‘Jackmanii Superba’ – larger, darker blooms
  • Related Jackman hybrids in purple and blue tones

Symbolism

Represents ambition, creativity, and ornamental beauty in garden design.
