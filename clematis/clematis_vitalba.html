Clematis vitalba – Old Man’s Beard

Clematis vitalba is a vigorous climbing vine known for its fluffy seed heads that give rise to the name “old man’s beard.”

Description

A deciduous, woody climber producing small creamy-white, star-shaped flowers followed by long, feathery seed heads.

Etymology

“Vitalba” comes from Latin meaning “white vine,” referring to its pale flowers and seed heads.

Taxonomy

Family: Ranunculaceae
Genus: Clematis
Species: C. vitalba

Distribution and Habitat

Native to Europe and western Asia, commonly found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and scrublands.

Ecology

Provides shelter for insects and birds, though it can become invasive in some regions due to rapid growth.

Cultivation

  • Full sun to partial shade
  • Moist, well-drained soil
  • Requires support for climbing
  • Prune to control spread

Cultivars

  • Mainly wild species in cultivation

Symbolism

Represents wildness, untamed growth, and natural abundance.
