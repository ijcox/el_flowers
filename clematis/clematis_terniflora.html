Clematis terniflora – Sweet Autumn Clematis

Clematis terniflora is a late-blooming, vigorous climbing vine known for its profusion of fragrant white flowers in late summer and autumn.

Description

A deciduous, fast-growing vine producing masses of small, star-shaped white flowers with a strong sweet fragrance, followed by fluffy seed heads.

Etymology

“Terniflora” means “three-flowered,” referring to the typical arrangement of its flower clusters.

Taxonomy

Family: Ranunculaceae
Genus: Clematis
Species: C. terniflora

Distribution and Habitat

Native to East Asia, especially Japan and China, and widely naturalized in temperate regions.

Ecology

Provides late-season nectar for pollinators, though it can be highly vigorous and spread aggressively in some environments.

Cultivation

  • Full sun to partial shade
  • Well-drained soil
  • Requires strong support for climbing
  • Prune to control spread after flowering

Cultivars

  • Mainly the wild form is cultivated

Symbolism

Represents late beauty, persistence, and abundance at the end of the growing season.
