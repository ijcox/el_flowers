Heliotropium peruvianum – Peruvian Heliotrope

Heliotrope peruvianum, commonly known as Peruvian Heliotrope, is a fragrant ornamental plant prized for its dense clusters of violet flowers.

Description

This species forms compact shrubs with richly scented purple to deep violet flower clusters and textured dark green foliage.

Etymology

The name “peruvianum” indicates its origin in Peru and surrounding regions of South America.

Taxonomy

Family: Boraginaceae
Genus: Heliotropium
Species: H. peruvianum

Distribution and Habitat

Native to Peru and adjacent areas, it is widely cultivated in temperate regions as a garden ornamental.

Ecology

Its strong fragrance attracts bees and butterflies, making it a valuable pollinator-friendly plant in gardens.

Cultivation

  • Full sun preferred
  • Well-drained fertile soil
  • Regular watering during growth
  • Often grown as an annual in cooler climates

Cultivars

  • Deep violet garden forms
  • Dwarf compact varieties
  • Highly fragrant selections

Symbolism

Represents devotion, attraction, and enduring affection due to its fragrance and vivid color.
