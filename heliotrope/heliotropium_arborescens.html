Heliotropium arborescens – Garden Heliotrope

Heliotrope arborescens, commonly known as Garden Heliotrope, is a fragrant flowering shrub admired for its clustered purple blooms and sweet vanilla-like scent.

Description

This plant produces dense clusters of small purple to violet flowers, often highly fragrant, on bushy stems with dark green foliage.

Etymology

“Heliotropium” comes from Greek meaning “turning toward the sun,” reflecting the perceived sun-tracking behavior of some species.

Taxonomy

Family: Boraginaceae
Genus: Heliotropium
Species: H. arborescens

Distribution and Habitat

Native to Peru and surrounding regions of South America, it is widely cultivated worldwide as an ornamental plant.

Ecology

Attracts bees, butterflies, and other pollinators, especially due to its strong fragrance.

Cultivation

  • Full sun to partial shade
  • Well-drained fertile soil
  • Regular watering but avoid waterlogging
  • Often grown as an annual in cooler climates

Cultivars

  • ‘Marine’ – deep violet flowers
  • ‘White Lady’ – white-flowered variety
  • Compact dwarf garden forms

Symbolism

Represents devotion, eternal love, and remembrance due to its fragrance and sun-following associations.
