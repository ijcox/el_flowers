Heliotropium curassavicum – Salt Heliotrope

Heliotrope curassavicum, commonly known as Salt Heliotrope, is a low-growing coastal plant adapted to saline environments.

Description

This species forms spreading mats with fleshy leaves and small white to pale lavender flowers clustered along curved stems.

Etymology

“Curassavicum” refers to Curaçao in the Caribbean, where the species was early documented.

Taxonomy

Family: Boraginaceae
Genus: Heliotropium
Species: H. curassavicum

Distribution and Habitat

Native to the Americas, especially coastal and saline regions, it grows in salt marshes, beaches, and disturbed coastal soils.

Ecology

Important in stabilizing sandy and saline soils and providing nectar for small coastal pollinators.

Cultivation

  • Full sun required
  • Highly salt-tolerant
  • Prefers sandy or coastal soils
  • Drought tolerant once established

Cultivars

  • Wild creeping coastal form
  • Naturalized shoreline populations

Symbolism

Represents endurance and adaptability in harsh coastal environments.
