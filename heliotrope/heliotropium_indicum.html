Heliotropium indicum – Indian Heliotrope

Heliotrope indicum, commonly known as Indian Heliotrope, is a widespread wild plant recognized for its coiled flower spikes and small purple blooms.

Description

This species produces rough, hairy stems with small violet to lavender flowers arranged in curved, scorpion-like clusters.

Etymology

“Indicum” refers to its presence in India and tropical regions of Asia.

Taxonomy

Family: Boraginaceae
Genus: Heliotropium
Species: H. indicum

Distribution and Habitat

Native to tropical Asia but now widespread in warm regions worldwide, often found in disturbed soils, roadsides, and fields.

Ecology

Provides nectar for small insects and is considered an important pioneer species in disturbed habitats.

Cultivation

  • Full sun preferred
  • Tolerates poor soils
  • Low water requirements once established
  • Often considered a wild or weedy plant

Cultivars

  • Wild natural forms
  • Rare ornamental selections

Symbolism

Represents resilience and persistence due to its ability to thrive in disturbed environments.
