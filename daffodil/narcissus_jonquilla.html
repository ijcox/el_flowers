Narcissus jonquilla – Jonquil

Daffodil jonquilla is a fragrant species known for its slender leaves and clusters of small yellow flowers.

Description

A perennial bulb with narrow, rush-like leaves and multiple sweetly scented yellow flowers per stem.

Etymology

“Jonquilla” comes from Spanish “jonquillo,” meaning “little rush,” referring to its thin leaves.

Taxonomy

Family: Amaryllidaceae
Genus: Narcissus
Species: N. jonquilla

Distribution and Habitat

Native to the Iberian Peninsula, especially Spain and Portugal, growing in meadows and open fields.

Ecology

Highly attractive to pollinators due to its strong fragrance and clustered blooms.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Thrives in warm climates
  • Suitable for naturalizing

Cultivars

  • ‘Baby Moon’ – miniature yellow flowers
  • Hybrid jonquil varieties

Symbolism

Represents affection, desire, and warmth.
