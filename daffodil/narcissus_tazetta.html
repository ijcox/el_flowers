Narcissus tazetta – Bunch-flowered Daffodil

Daffodil tazetta is known for producing clusters of small, fragrant flowers on a single stem.

Description

A perennial bulb forming multiple white or cream flowers per stem, each with a small central cup, often strongly scented.

Etymology

“Tazetta” refers to a small cup, describing the shape of its corona.

Taxonomy

Family: Amaryllidaceae
Genus: Narcissus
Species: N. tazetta

Distribution and Habitat

Native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia, commonly found in dry, open habitats.

Ecology

Pollinated by insects attracted to its fragrance, especially in mild winter and early spring climates.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Prefers warmer climates
  • Often forced indoors for winter blooms

Cultivars

  • ‘Paperwhite’ – popular indoor flowering variety
  • ‘Grand Soleil d’Or’ – yellow and orange tones

Symbolism

Represents abundance, joy, and renewal.
