Narcissus bulbocodium – Hoop Petticoat Daffodil

Daffodil bulbocodium is a unique species known for its large, flared trumpet and minimal petals, resembling a hoop petticoat.

Description

A small bulbous perennial with a dominant funnel-shaped corona and very reduced outer tepals, creating a distinctive bell-like form.

Etymology

“Bulbocodium” refers to its bulbous growth habit and characteristic floral shape.

Taxonomy

Family: Amaryllidaceae
Genus: Narcissus
Species: N. bulbocodium

Distribution and Habitat

Native to southwestern Europe and North Africa, often found in grasslands and open rocky habitats.

Ecology

Early flowering and adapted to seasonal climates, supporting pollinators in early spring.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Ideal for rock gardens
  • Requires dry summer dormancy

Cultivars

  • Various yellow and pale cream forms

Symbolism

Represents individuality, charm, and delicate beauty.
