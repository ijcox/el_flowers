Narcissus pseudonarcissus – Wild Daffodil

Daffodil pseudonarcissus is the classic wild daffodil species, widely recognized for its bright yellow trumpet-shaped flowers.

Description

A perennial bulb producing nodding yellow flowers with a central trumpet (corona) surrounded by six petal-like tepals.

Etymology

“Pseudonarcissus” means “false narcissus,” historically distinguishing it within the genus.

Taxonomy

Family: Amaryllidaceae
Genus: Narcissus
Species: N. pseudonarcissus

Distribution and Habitat

Native to western Europe, especially meadows, woodlands, and river valleys.

Ecology

An early spring bloomer providing nectar for pollinators emerging after winter.

Cultivation

  • Full sun to partial shade
  • Well-drained soil
  • Naturalizes well in grasslands
  • Plant bulbs in autumn

Cultivars

  • Numerous garden forms derived from this species

Symbolism

Represents renewal, rebirth, and the arrival of spring.
