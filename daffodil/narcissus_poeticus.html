Narcissus poeticus – Poet’s Daffodil

Daffodil poeticus is a fragrant, elegant species known for its white petals and small, red-edged central cup.

Description

A perennial bulb producing pure white flowers with a short yellow corona edged in red, often highly fragrant.

Etymology

“Poeticus” reflects its long association with poetry, mythology, and classical literature.

Taxonomy

Family: Amaryllidaceae
Genus: Narcissus
Species: N. poeticus

Distribution and Habitat

Native to central and southern Europe, commonly found in meadows and grasslands.

Ecology

Late spring flowering provides nectar for pollinators after many early bloomers have faded.

Cultivation

  • Full sun to partial shade
  • Moist, well-drained soil
  • Suitable for naturalizing
  • Plant bulbs in autumn

Cultivars

  • ‘Actaea’ – classic fragrant form

Symbolism

Represents inspiration, beauty, and artistic expression.
