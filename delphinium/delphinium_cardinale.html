Delphinium consolida – Field Larkspur

Delphinium consolida is an annual wildflower known for its airy spikes of blue to purple flowers in open fields.

Description

An annual plant with finely divided foliage and slender flower spikes bearing small, delicate blossoms in shades of blue, pink, or white.

Etymology

“Consolida” refers to its historical medicinal use in wound healing.

Taxonomy

Family: Ranunculaceae
Genus: Delphinium
Species: D. consolida

Distribution and Habitat

Native to Europe and western Asia, typically found in arable fields and disturbed soils.

Ecology

Provides nectar for bees and butterflies in agricultural landscapes.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Often grown as a wildflower mix component
  • Self-seeding annual

Cultivars

  • Modern garden selections in mixed colors

Symbolism

Represents lightness, remembrance, and natural beauty.
