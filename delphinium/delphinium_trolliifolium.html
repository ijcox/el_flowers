Delphinium cardinale – Scarlet Larkspur

Delphinium cardinale is a striking species known for its vivid scarlet-red flowers, uncommon among delphiniums.

Description

A tall perennial producing upright spikes of bright red to orange-red flowers, attracting hummingbirds and pollinators.

Etymology

“Cardinale” refers to its cardinal-red coloration.

Taxonomy

Family: Ranunculaceae
Genus: Delphinium
Species: D. cardinale

Distribution and Habitat

Native to California and Baja California, growing in dry, chaparral-like habitats.

Ecology

Specialized pollination by hummingbirds due to its red tubular flowers.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Well-drained, dry soil
  • Drought tolerant once established
  • Best in Mediterranean climates

Cultivars

  • Primarily wild species form in cultivation

Symbolism

Represents passion, intensity, and vitality.
