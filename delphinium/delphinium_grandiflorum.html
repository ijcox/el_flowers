Delphinium grandiflorum – Siberian Larkspur

Delphinium grandiflorum is a compact species known for its vivid blue flowers and bushy growth habit.

Description

A low-growing perennial producing finely divided foliage and bright blue, violet, or occasionally white flowers.

Etymology

“Grandiflorum” means “large-flowered,” despite the species being more compact than many delphiniums.

Taxonomy

Family: Ranunculaceae
Genus: Delphinium
Species: D. grandiflorum

Distribution and Habitat

Native to Siberia, Mongolia, and northern China, growing in grasslands and rocky slopes.

Ecology

Attracts bees and other pollinators, especially in open sunny habitats.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Drought tolerant once established
  • Short-lived perennial in some climates

Cultivars

  • ‘Blue Butterfly’ – compact ornamental form

Symbolism

Represents lightness, joy, and freedom.
