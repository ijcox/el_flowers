Geranium pratense – Meadow Cranesbill

Geranium pratense is a tall, hardy perennial known for its striking blue-purple flowers in meadow habitats.

Description

A clump-forming plant producing large, open flowers in shades of blue, violet, or lilac during summer.

Etymology

“Pratense” means “of meadows,” reflecting its natural habitat.

Taxonomy

Family: Geraniaceae
Genus: Geranium
Species: G. pratense

Distribution and Habitat

Native to Europe and Asia, commonly found in meadows, grasslands, and roadside verges.

Ecology

Attracts bees and butterflies and thrives in open sunny environments.

Cultivation

  • Full sun to partial shade
  • Well-drained soil
  • Hardy perennial
  • Ideal for naturalistic planting

Cultivars

  • ‘Mrs Kendall Clark’ – pale blue flowers
  • ‘Johnson’s Blue’ – deep violet blooms

Symbolism

Represents openness, wild beauty, and meadow tranquility.
