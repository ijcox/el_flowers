Geranium maculatum – Wild Geranium

Geranium maculatum is a woodland perennial known for its delicate pink-purple flowers and medicinal history.

Description

A clump-forming herbaceous plant producing five-petaled lavender to pink flowers above deeply lobed leaves.

Etymology

“Maculatum” means “spotted,” referring to markings found on parts of the plant in some specimens.

Taxonomy

Family: Geraniaceae
Genus: Geranium
Species: G. maculatum

Distribution and Habitat

Native to eastern North America, found in deciduous woodlands and shaded slopes.

Ecology

Attracts bees and other pollinators; thrives in shaded forest environments.

Cultivation

  • Partial to full shade
  • Moist, well-drained soil
  • Woodland garden plant
  • Low maintenance perennial

Cultivars

  • Mainly wild-type in cultivation

Symbolism

Represents healing, woodland serenity, and natural resilience.
