Geranium sylvaticum – Wood Cranesbill

Geranium sylvaticum is a woodland perennial known for its soft violet-blue flowers and natural woodland charm.

Description

A clump-forming herb producing delicate purple-blue flowers above finely divided leaves.

Etymology

“Sylvaticum” means “of the woods,” referring to its woodland habitat.

Taxonomy

Family: Geraniaceae
Genus: Geranium
Species: G. sylvaticum

Distribution and Habitat

Native to Europe and parts of Asia, commonly found in woodland edges and meadows.

Ecology

Provides nectar for bees and pollinators in shaded habitats.

Cultivation

  • Partial shade
  • Moist, well-drained soil
  • Hardy perennial
  • Suitable for woodland gardens

Cultivars

  • ‘Mayflower’ – soft blue flowers

Symbolism

Represents woodland serenity, gentleness, and natural balance.
