Geranium sanguineum – Bloody Cranesbill

Geranium sanguineum is a low-growing perennial known for its vibrant magenta flowers and spreading habit.

Description

A mat-forming plant producing vivid pink to purplish flowers with deeply divided leaves.

Etymology

“Sanguineum” refers to “blood-red,” describing the flower’s intense coloration.

Taxonomy

Family: Geraniaceae
Genus: Geranium
Species: G. sanguineum

Distribution and Habitat

Native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in rocky grasslands and coastal areas.

Ecology

Attracts bees and butterflies; thrives in dry, sunny conditions.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Drought tolerant
  • Excellent ground cover plant

Cultivars

  • ‘Max Frei’ – compact bright form
  • ‘Album’ – white-flowered variety

Symbolism

Represents vitality, resilience, and bold beauty.
