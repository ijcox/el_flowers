Hydrangea arborescens – Smooth Hydrangea

Hydrangea arborescens, commonly known as Smooth Hydrangea, is a hardy North American shrub known for its large white flower clusters and easy garden performance.

Description

This species produces rounded clusters of white flowers on soft woody stems with broad green leaves, often forming a loose shrub.

Etymology

“Arborescens” means “tree-like,” referring to its woody shrub growth habit.

Taxonomy

Family: Hydrangeaceae
Genus: Hydrangea
Species: H. arborescens

Distribution and Habitat

Native to the eastern United States, it grows in woodland edges, slopes, and moist forests.

Ecology

Attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies and provides seasonal garden structure.

Cultivation

  • Partial shade to full sun
  • Moist, well-drained soil
  • Very cold hardy
  • Can be cut back hard in late winter

Cultivars

  • ‘Annabelle’ – large snowball white flowers
  • ‘Incrediball’ – stronger stems and larger blooms

Symbolism

Represents purity, grace, and abundance.
