Hydrangea macrophylla – Bigleaf Hydrangea

Hydrangea macrophylla, commonly known as Bigleaf Hydrangea, is a popular ornamental shrub known for its large flower heads and ability to change color depending on soil pH.

Description

This species produces large rounded or lacecap flower clusters in shades of pink, blue, purple, or white, with broad serrated leaves.

Etymology

“Macrophylla” means “large-leaved,” referring to its broad foliage.

Taxonomy

Family: Hydrangeaceae
Genus: Hydrangea
Species: H. macrophylla

Distribution and Habitat

Native to Japan, it is widely cultivated in temperate gardens around the world, preferring sheltered woodland edges.

Ecology

Provides nectar and pollen for pollinators and is an important ornamental shrub in garden ecosystems.

Cultivation

  • Partial shade preferred
  • Moist, well-drained soil
  • Soil pH affects flower color
  • Protect from harsh frost in colder climates

Cultivars

  • ‘Endless Summer’ – repeat blooming variety
  • ‘Nikko Blue’ – deep blue flowers
  • ‘Forever Pink’ – pink flower selection

Symbolism

Represents gratitude, understanding, and heartfelt emotion.
