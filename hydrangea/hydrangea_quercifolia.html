Hydrangea quercifolia – Oakleaf Hydrangea

Hydrangea quercifolia, commonly known as Oakleaf Hydrangea, is a distinctive North American shrub known for its oak-shaped leaves and dramatic seasonal color changes.

Description

This species produces large cone-shaped white flower clusters and deeply lobed leaves that resemble oak foliage, turning red, orange, and purple in autumn.

Etymology

“Quercifolia” means “oak-leaved,” referring to its uniquely shaped foliage.

Taxonomy

Family: Hydrangeaceae
Genus: Hydrangea
Species: H. quercifolia

Distribution and Habitat

Native to the southeastern United States, it grows in woodland areas, slopes, and ravines.

Ecology

Provides nectar for pollinators and supports wildlife through seasonal cover and structure.

Cultivation

  • Partial shade preferred
  • Well-drained, organic-rich soil
  • Moderate drought tolerance once established
  • Excellent fall color interest

Cultivars

  • ‘Snow Queen’ – upright flower form
  • ‘Alice’ – large vigorous shrub

Symbolism

Represents endurance, transformation, and natural beauty.
