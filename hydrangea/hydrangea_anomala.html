Hydrangea anomala – Climbing Hydrangea

Hydrangea anomala, commonly known as Climbing Hydrangea, is a vigorous woody vine that climbs walls and trees using aerial rootlets.

Description

This species produces flat lacecap-style white flower clusters and heart-shaped leaves, growing as a self-clinging climber.

Etymology

“Anomala” means “irregular” or “unusual,” referring to its climbing habit compared to typical hydrangeas.

Taxonomy

Family: Hydrangeaceae
Genus: Hydrangea
Species: H. anomala

Distribution and Habitat

Native to woodlands of the Himalayas and parts of Asia, it thrives in shaded forest environments.

Ecology

Provides nectar for pollinators and offers habitat cover as it climbs and spreads across surfaces.

Cultivation

  • Partial shade preferred
  • Moist, well-drained soil
  • Slow to establish but long-lived
  • Requires support structures or surfaces

Cultivars

  • ‘Petiolaris’ – most common garden form
  • Japanese woodland selections

Symbolism

Represents perseverance, attachment, and steady growth.
