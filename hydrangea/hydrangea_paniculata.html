Hydrangea paniculata – Panicle Hydrangea

Hydrangea paniculata, commonly known as Panicle Hydrangea, is a hardy ornamental shrub valued for its large cone-shaped flower clusters and strong cold tolerance.

Description

This species produces elongated conical flower panicles that begin white or cream and often turn pink as they age, supported by sturdy upright branches.

Etymology

“Paniculata” refers to the plant’s panicled (branched cluster) flower structure.

Taxonomy

Family: Hydrangeaceae
Genus: Hydrangea
Species: H. paniculata

Distribution and Habitat

Native to China, Japan, and Korea, it grows in forest edges and mountainous regions and is widely cultivated in temperate gardens.

Ecology

Provides nectar for pollinators and is especially valuable for late-summer garden interest.

Cultivation

  • Full sun to partial shade
  • Adaptable to many soil types
  • Very cold hardy shrub
  • Can be pruned for larger flower heads

Cultivars

  • ‘Limelight’ – lime-green flowers turning pink
  • ‘Pinky Winky’ – bi-colored blooms
  • ‘Grandiflora’ – classic white panicles

Symbolism

Represents grace, abundance, and perseverance.
