Symphyotrichum novae-angliae – New England Aster

Aster novae-angliae is a tall, late-blooming North American wildflower known for its dense purple flower clusters.

Description

Produces tall stems covered in small purple, pink, or occasionally white daisy-like flowers with yellow centers.

Etymology

“Novae-angliae” means “of New England,” referring to its native range in northeastern North America.

Taxonomy

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Symphyotrichum
Species: S. novae-angliae

Distribution and Habitat

Native to eastern and central North America, commonly found in meadows, prairies, and roadside habitats.

Ecology

A critical late-season nectar source for bees, butterflies, and migrating pollinators.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Moist, well-drained soil
  • Benefits from deadheading

Cultivars

  • ‘Purple Dome’
  • ‘Harrington’s Pink’

Symbolism

Represents patience, remembrance, and autumn resilience.
