Callistephus chinensis – China Aster

Aster chinensis is an ornamental annual flower widely cultivated for its large, colorful blooms and decorative garden value.

Description

Produces showy, fully double or semi-double flowers in a wide range of colors including pink, purple, red, white, and blue.

Etymology

“Callistephus” comes from Greek meaning “beautiful crown,” while “chinensis” refers to its origin in China.

Taxonomy

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Callistephus
Species: C. chinensis

Distribution and Habitat

Native to China and Korea, now widely cultivated worldwide in gardens and floral industries.

Ecology

Attracts bees and butterflies, though primarily grown as an ornamental rather than wild species.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Well-drained fertile soil
  • Grown from seed annually
  • Regular deadheading encourages blooming

Cultivars

  • ‘Pompon Mixed’
  • ‘Princess Series’
  • ‘Milady Series’

Symbolism

Represents patience, elegance, and enduring love in floral traditions.
