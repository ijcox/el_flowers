Aster Alpinus – Alpine Aster

Aster alpinus is a low-growing perennial wildflower native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

Description

Forms compact clumps with single daisy-like flowers in purple, blue, pink, or white with yellow centers.

Etymology

“Alpinus” refers to its natural alpine mountain habitat.

Taxonomy

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Aster (sensu lato)
Species: A. alpinus

Distribution and Habitat

Native to mountainous regions of Europe, Asia, and North America. Found in rocky alpine meadows.

Ecology

Important early-season nectar source for alpine pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Well-drained rocky or sandy soil
  • Drought tolerant

Cultivars

  • ‘Albus’
  • ‘Dark Beauty’

Symbolism

Represents endurance, resilience, and high-altitude beauty.
