Eurybia divaricata – White Wood Aster

Aster divaricata is a delicate woodland perennial known for its airy sprays of white flowers and dark stems.

Description

Produces small white star-like flowers with yellow centers on branching dark stems. Leaves are heart-shaped and toothed.

Etymology

“Divaricata” means spreading or widely branched, referring to its loose flower structure.

Taxonomy

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Eurybia
Species: E. divaricata

Distribution and Habitat

Native to eastern North America, especially woodland understories and shaded slopes.

Ecology

Important nectar source for late-summer woodland pollinators including bees and small butterflies.

Cultivation

  • Partial to full shade
  • Moist, well-drained soil
  • Spreads slowly in ideal conditions

Cultivars

  • Mainly wild-type in cultivation

Symbolism

Represents quiet strength, subtle beauty, and woodland serenity.
