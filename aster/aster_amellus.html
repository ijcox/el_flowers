Aster Amellus – Italian Aster

Aster amellus is a late-summer perennial known for its star-shaped violet-blue flowers native to Europe.

Description

Compact perennial with daisy-like purple to blue flowers and yellow centers. Upright stems form clumps.

Etymology

“Aster” comes from Greek meaning “star,” referring to the flower shape.

Taxonomy

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Aster (sensu lato)
Species: A. amellus

Distribution and Habitat

Native to central and southern Europe, growing in dry grasslands and rocky slopes.

Ecology

Provides nectar for bees and butterflies during late summer bloom periods.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Drought tolerant once established

Cultivars

  • ‘King George’
  • ‘Veilchenkönigin’

Symbolism

Represents elegance, patience, and late-season beauty.
