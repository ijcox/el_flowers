Verbena

Verbena is a group of flowering plants known for their clusters of small, colorful blooms and long flowering season.

Description

Verbena flowers grow in dense clusters and appear in purple, pink, red, white, and blue shades. Plants may be upright or trailing depending on species.

They are widely used in hanging baskets, borders, and pollinator gardens.

Taxonomy

Verbena belongs to the genus Verbena in the family Verbenaceae.

Species

Verbena Officinalis – Common Verbena
Verbena Bonariensis – Tall Verbena
Verbena Hastata – Blue Vervain

Etymology

The name Verbena comes from Latin, historically referring to sacred plants used in rituals.

Ecology

Verbena attracts bees, butterflies, and other pollinators and thrives in sunny, open habitats.

Diseases and pests

  • Powdery mildew in humid climates
  • Aphids on flower stems
  • Spider mites in dry heat
  • Root rot in poorly drained soil

Cultivation

Verbena is widely grown for long-lasting color in gardens and containers.

  • Light: Full sun
  • Soil: Well-drained soil
  • Water: Moderate to low
  • Planting: Seeds or cuttings
  • Maintenance: Trim for continuous blooming

Verbena in culture

Verbena symbolizes healing, creativity, and protection in various traditions.
