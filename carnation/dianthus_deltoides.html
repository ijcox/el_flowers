Dianthus deltoides – Maiden Pink

Carnation deltoides, known as maiden pink, is a delicate wildflower commonly found in dry grasslands and meadows.

Description

A low-growing perennial forming spreading mats of narrow leaves and producing small, five-petaled pink to magenta flowers with a darker central ring.

Etymology

“Deltoides” refers to the triangular shape of its leaves, resembling the Greek letter delta.

Taxonomy

Family: Caryophyllaceae
Genus: Dianthus
Species: D. deltoides

Distribution and Habitat

Native to Europe and western Asia, typically found in dry grasslands, heathlands, and open woodland edges.

Ecology

Provides nectar for butterflies and bees, and thrives in nutrient-poor soils where competition is low.

Cultivation

  • Prefers full sun
  • Well-drained, sandy or gravelly soil
  • Drought tolerant once established
  • Excellent ground cover plant

Cultivars

  • ‘Flashing Light’ – bright pink blooms
  • ‘Alba’ – white flowering form
  • ‘Zing Rose’ – vivid magenta selection

Symbolism

Represents modest beauty, resilience, and natural simplicity.
