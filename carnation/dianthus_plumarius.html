Dianthus plumarius – Cottage Pink

Carnation plumarius, known as cottage pink, is a fragrant perennial widely grown in rock gardens and borders.

Description

A low-growing perennial forming clumps of slender grey-green leaves and producing highly fragrant, fringed flowers in pink, white, and lilac tones.

Etymology

“Plumarius” refers to the feather-like, finely divided petals of the flowers.

Taxonomy

Family: Caryophyllaceae
Genus: Dianthus
Species: D. plumarius

Distribution and Habitat

Native to central and southern Europe, commonly found in rocky grasslands and well-drained slopes.

Ecology

Attracts bees and butterflies with its strong fragrance and nectar-rich flowers.

Cultivation

  • Prefers full sun
  • Well-drained, slightly alkaline soil
  • Drought tolerant once established
  • Ideal for rock gardens and borders

Cultivars

  • ‘Mrs Sinkins’ – classic white, highly fragrant form
  • ‘Rose de Mai’ – soft pink heirloom variety
  • ‘Inelegance’ series – modern mixed colors

Symbolism

Represents admiration and refined beauty, often associated with traditional cottage gardens.
