Dianthus caryophyllus – Carnation

Carnation caryophyllus is the classic carnation species widely cultivated for its fragrant, ruffled flowers used in bouquets and floral arrangements.

Description

A perennial plant with narrow grey-green leaves and strongly scented flowers that range in color from white and pink to red and yellow. Blooms are typically fringed or ruffled.

Etymology

“Dianthus” means “flower of the gods,” while “caryophyllus” refers historically to clove-like fragrance.

Taxonomy

Family: Caryophyllaceae
Genus: Dianthus
Species: D. caryophyllus

Distribution and Habitat

Native to the Mediterranean region, commonly cultivated worldwide in gardens and greenhouses.

Ecology

Attracts pollinators such as butterflies and bees. Widely grown for ornamental and commercial cut-flower use.

Cultivation

  • Prefers full sun
  • Well-drained, slightly alkaline soil
  • Regular deadheading encourages blooms
  • Often grown in greenhouses for cut flowers

Cultivars

  • ‘Chabaud’ series – highly fragrant, double flowers
  • ‘Grenadin’ series – long-stemmed cut flowers
  • ‘Malmaison’ – historic fragrant variety

Symbolism

Represents love, fascination, and distinction, commonly used in ceremonial bouquets.
