Dianthus chinensis – China Pink

Carnation chinensis, known as China pink, is a compact ornamental flower widely grown for its bright, patterned blooms.

Description

A short-lived perennial or annual with slender green leaves and brightly colored flowers often marked with contrasting edges or patterns in pink, red, white, and purple.

Etymology

“Chinensis” means “from China,” reflecting its native range and long history of cultivation in East Asia.

Taxonomy

Family: Caryophyllaceae
Genus: Dianthus
Species: D. chinensis

Distribution and Habitat

Native to northern China, Korea, Mongolia, and southeastern Russia, commonly found in grasslands and rocky slopes.

Ecology

Attracts bees and butterflies and is often used in ornamental bedding displays due to its vivid coloration.

Cultivation

  • Prefers full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Can be grown as an annual in cooler climates
  • Deadheading encourages longer blooming

Cultivars

  • ‘Telstar’ series – compact, colorful blooms
  • ‘Parfait’ series – bicolored flowers
  • ‘China Doll’ – mixed vibrant shades

Symbolism

Represents boldness, affection, and youthful energy, often used in decorative garden displays.
