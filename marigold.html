Marigold

Marigolds are bright, hardy flowering plants known for their vibrant orange and yellow blooms and strong pest-repelling properties.

Description

Marigold flowers are dense, rounded blooms with layered petals that range from golden yellow to deep orange and red tones.

They are widely used in gardens, borders, and cultural ceremonies.

Taxonomy

Marigolds belong to the genus Tagetes in the family Asteraceae. They are native to the Americas.

Species

Tagetes Erecta – African Marigold
Tagetes Patula – French Marigold
Tagetes Tenuifolia – Signet Marigold

Etymology

The name Tagetes comes from the Etruscan god Tages, symbolizing beauty and brightness.

Ecology

Marigolds attract pollinators while also repelling harmful insects such as nematodes and aphids.

Diseases and pests

  • Powdery mildew in humid conditions
  • Aphids on young shoots
  • Spider mites in dry climates
  • Root rot in poorly drained soil

Cultivation

Marigolds are widely grown as companion plants in vegetable gardens and ornamental beds.

  • Light: Full sun
  • Soil: Well-drained soil
  • Water: Moderate
  • Planting: Seeds sown in spring
  • Maintenance: Deadhead for continuous bloom

Marigold in culture

Marigolds symbolize warmth, celebration, and remembrance and are heavily used in festivals such as Día de los Muertos.
