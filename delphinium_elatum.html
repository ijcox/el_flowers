Delphinium elatum – Alpine Delphinium

Delphinium elatum is a tall, elegant perennial known for its striking spikes of deep blue flowers.

Description

A perennial herb producing tall flower spikes covered in densely packed blossoms, typically in shades of blue, purple, or white.

Etymology

“Elatum” means “tall” or “elevated,” referring to its upright flowering spikes.

Taxonomy

Family: Ranunculaceae
Genus: Delphinium
Species: D. elatum

Distribution and Habitat

Native to central and eastern Europe and parts of Asia, often found in alpine meadows and mountainous regions.

Ecology

Provides nectar for bees, especially long-tongued species capable of accessing deep flowers.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Rich, well-drained soil
  • Requires staking due to height
  • Prefers cool summer climates

Cultivars

  • ‘Pacific Giant’ series – large hybrid forms

Symbolism

Represents dignity, grace, and openness to new possibilities.
