Dahlia merckii – Pink Wild Dahlia

Dahlia merckii is a graceful wild species known for its delicate pink flowers and airy growth habit.

Description

A tuberous perennial producing single, light pink to lavender flowers with open centers and slender stems.

Etymology

Named after German botanist Carl Merck, contributing to botanical research in the 19th century.

Taxonomy

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Dahlia
Species: D. merckii

Distribution and Habitat

Native to Mexico, typically found in mountainous regions and open woodlands.

Ecology

Attracts bees and butterflies and contributes to the diversity of wild dahlia species.

Cultivation

  • Full sun to partial shade
  • Well-drained soil
  • Moderate watering
  • Suitable for naturalistic plantings

Cultivars

  • Mostly grown in wild-type form

Symbolism

Represents grace, softness, and natural elegance.
