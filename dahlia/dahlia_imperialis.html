Dahlia imperialis – Tree Dahlia

Dahlia imperialis is a tall, dramatic species known for its tree-like height and late-season blooms.

Description

A large perennial plant that can grow several meters tall, producing tall hollow stems and pale pink to lavender flowers high above the ground.

Etymology

“Imperialis” means “imperial” or “majestic,” reflecting its impressive size and stature.

Taxonomy

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Dahlia
Species: D. imperialis

Distribution and Habitat

Native to Mexico and Central America, often found in mountainous regions and forest edges.

Ecology

Provides nectar for pollinators late in the growing season when fewer plants are in bloom.

Cultivation

  • Full sun to partial shade
  • Rich, well-drained soil
  • Requires protection from frost
  • Needs space due to large size

Cultivars

  • Mostly grown in its natural form

Symbolism

Represents grandeur, elegance, and strength.
