Dahlia variabilis – Common Hybrid Dahlia

Dahlia variabilis refers to the diverse group of cultivated hybrid dahlias widely grown in gardens for their dramatic and varied flowers.

Description

A tuberous perennial group producing an extraordinary range of flower forms, sizes, and colors, from small single blooms to large decorative doubles.

Etymology

“Variabilis” means “variable,” highlighting the immense diversity within cultivated dahlias.

Taxonomy

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Dahlia
Species: D. variabilis (horticultural classification)

Distribution and Habitat

Derived from Mexican species, now cultivated worldwide in gardens and floral displays.

Ecology

Highly attractive to pollinators, especially single-flowered varieties.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Fertile, well-drained soil
  • Regular watering and feeding
  • Tubers lifted in colder climates

Cultivars

  • Thousands of named garden varieties across multiple flower types

Symbolism

Represents diversity, creativity, and elegance.
