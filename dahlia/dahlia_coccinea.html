Dahlia coccinea – Wild Dahlia

Dahlia coccinea is a naturally occurring species known for its bright, simple flowers and importance in dahlia hybridization.

Description

A tuberous perennial with single, open flowers typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow, surrounding a central disc.

Etymology

“Coccinea” means “scarlet,” referring to its vivid red coloration.

Taxonomy

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Dahlia
Species: D. coccinea

Distribution and Habitat

Native to Mexico and Central America, commonly found in open woodlands and mountainous regions.

Ecology

Supports pollinators such as bees and butterflies and contributes to genetic diversity in cultivated dahlias.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Moderate watering needs
  • Hardier than many cultivated forms

Cultivars

  • Used in breeding programs rather than widely cultivated varieties

Symbolism

Represents vitality, energy, and natural beauty.
