Dahlia pinnata – Garden Dahlia

Dahlia pinnata is one of the primary species behind modern cultivated dahlias, known for its diverse and showy blooms.

Description

A tuberous perennial producing large, brightly colored flowers in a wide range of forms, including single, double, and decorative types.

Etymology

“Pinnata” refers to its pinnate (feather-like) leaf arrangement.

Taxonomy

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Dahlia
Species: D. pinnata

Distribution and Habitat

Native to Mexico, growing in mountainous regions and open habitats.

Ecology

Attracts bees and butterflies, playing an important role in pollinator-friendly gardens.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Rich, well-drained soil
  • Regular watering during growth
  • Tubers lifted in cold climates

Cultivars

  • Thousands of hybrid varieties derived from this species

Symbolism

Represents elegance, creativity, and inner strength.
