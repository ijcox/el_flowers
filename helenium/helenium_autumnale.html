Helenium autumnale – Common Sneezeweed

Helenium autumnale, commonly known as Common Sneezeweed, is a late-summer flowering perennial known for its bright daisy-like blooms.

Description

This species produces upright stems topped with vibrant yellow to orange flower heads featuring prominent central disks and drooping ray petals.

Etymology

“Helenium” is linked to Helen of Troy in Greek mythology, while “autumnale” refers to its autumn flowering period.

Taxonomy

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Helenium
Species: H. autumnale

Distribution and Habitat

Native to North America, it grows in moist meadows, prairies, and along stream banks.

Ecology

Provides nectar for bees, butterflies, and other pollinators during late flowering seasons when fewer plants are in bloom.

Cultivation

  • Full sun preferred
  • Moist, fertile soil
  • Regular watering for best growth
  • May benefit from staking in rich soils

Cultivars

  • ‘Moerheim Beauty’ – deep red flowers
  • ‘Sahin’s Early Flowerer’ – extended blooming season
  • Mixed wild-type color forms

Symbolism

Represents resilience and warmth, bringing color to late-season gardens.
