Helenium bigelovii – Bigelow’s Sneezeweed

Helenium bigelovii, commonly known as Bigelow’s Sneezeweed, is a perennial wildflower native to western North America.

Description

This species produces tall stems with bright yellow daisy-like flowers and prominent central disks, typically blooming in mid to late summer.

Etymology

Named after botanist John Milton Bigelow, who contributed to the study of North American flora.

Taxonomy

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Helenium
Species: H. bigelovii

Distribution and Habitat

Native to mountainous regions of western North America, often found in moist meadows and stream edges.

Ecology

Attracts bees, butterflies, and other pollinators, playing an important role in meadow ecosystems.

Cultivation

  • Full sun to light shade
  • Moist, well-drained soil
  • Regular watering preferred
  • Cold hardy perennial

Cultivars

  • Wild golden form
  • Selected garden strains with larger blooms

Symbolism

Represents vitality and natural abundance in mountain meadow ecosystems.
