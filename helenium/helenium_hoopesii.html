Helenium hoopesii – Orange Sneezeweed

Helenium hoopesii, commonly known as Orange Sneezeweed, is a striking perennial species recognized for its vivid orange-yellow blooms.

Description

This species produces tall stems topped with large daisy-like flowers featuring bright orange ray petals surrounding a golden central cone.

Etymology

Named after amateur botanist H.C. Hoopes, who contributed to early documentation of the species.

Taxonomy

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Helenium
Species: H. hoopesii

Distribution and Habitat

Native to western North America, particularly mountainous regions, where it grows in alpine meadows and open forest clearings.

Ecology

Highly attractive to bees and butterflies, it plays an important role in high-altitude pollinator ecosystems.

Cultivation

  • Full sun preferred
  • Well-drained but moist soil
  • Cold hardy perennial
  • Benefits from regular watering in dry climates

Cultivars

  • Wild alpine form
  • Garden-selected larger flower strains

Symbolism

Represents brightness and endurance in harsh alpine environments.
