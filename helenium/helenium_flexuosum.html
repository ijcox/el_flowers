Helenium flexuosum – Purplehead Sneezeweed

Helenium flexuosum, commonly known as Purplehead Sneezeweed, is a perennial wildflower known for its distinctive darker central cones.

Description

This species produces slender stems topped with yellow daisy-like flowers and prominent purplish-brown central disks.

Etymology

“Flexuosum” refers to the plant’s somewhat bending or irregular stem growth habit.

Taxonomy

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Helenium
Species: H. flexuosum

Distribution and Habitat

Native to eastern North America, it grows in meadows, prairies, and open woodland edges.

Ecology

Provides nectar and pollen for bees, butterflies, and other insects during late summer bloom periods.

Cultivation

  • Full sun preferred
  • Moist to average soil
  • Tolerates a range of conditions
  • Low maintenance perennial

Cultivars

  • Wild prairie form
  • Selected ornamental strains

Symbolism

Represents adaptability and natural balance in prairie ecosystems.
