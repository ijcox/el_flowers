Campanula lactiflora – Milky Bellflower

Bellflower lactiflora is a tall perennial known for its airy clusters of pale blue to creamy-white flowers.

Description

Produces branching stems with many small bell-shaped flowers forming loose, cloud-like panicles.

Etymology

“Lactiflora” means “milk-flowered,” referring to its soft pale coloration.

Taxonomy

Family: Campanulaceae
Genus: Campanula
Species: C. lactiflora

Distribution and Habitat

Native to the Caucasus and western Asia, found in meadows and woodland edges.

Ecology

Attracts bees and butterflies; valued for extended summer blooming.

Cultivation

  • Full sun to partial shade
  • Moist, well-drained soil
  • Can benefit from staking due to height

Cultivars

  • ‘Loddon Anna’
  • ‘Pouffe’

Symbolism

Represents gratitude, openness, and gentle abundance.
