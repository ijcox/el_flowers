Campanula portenschlagiana – Dalmatian Bellflower

Bellflower portenschlagiana is a low-growing perennial often used in rock gardens and wall plantings for its trailing habit.

Description

Forms dense mats of small, star-shaped purple-blue flowers blooming abundantly in summer.

Etymology

Named after Franz von Portenschlag-Ledermayer, an Austrian botanist.

Taxonomy

Family: Campanulaceae
Genus: Campanula
Species: C. portenschlagiana

Distribution and Habitat

Native to the Dalmatian coast (Croatia), growing in rocky cliffs and limestone crevices.

Ecology

Attracts bees and small pollinators; thrives in harsh, rocky environments.

Cultivation

  • Full sun to partial shade
  • Well-drained soil
  • Drought tolerant once established

Cultivars

  • ‘Birch Hybrid’ selections

Symbolism

Represents endurance, humility, and quiet persistence.
