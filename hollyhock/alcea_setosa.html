Alcea setosa – Bristly Hollyhock

Hollyhock setosa, commonly known as Bristly Hollyhock, is a perennial species recognized for its hairy stems and vivid pink flowers.

Description

This species produces tall stems covered in coarse bristles, with bright pink to magenta funnel-shaped flowers arranged along the upper portion of the spike.

Etymology

“Setosa” means “bristly,” referring to the plant’s hairy stems and leaves.

Taxonomy

Family: Malvaceae
Genus: Alcea
Species: A. setosa

Distribution and Habitat

Native to the eastern Mediterranean and Middle East, it grows in dry rocky hillsides and scrublands.

Ecology

Attracts bees and butterflies and is well adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Cultivation

  • Full sun preferred
  • Well-drained, rocky soil
  • Drought tolerant once established
  • Hardy perennial in warm climates

Cultivars

  • Wild pink form
  • Ornamental garden selections

Symbolism

Represents strength and resilience in arid landscapes.
