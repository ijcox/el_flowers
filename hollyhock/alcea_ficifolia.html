Alcea ficifolia – Fig-leaved Hollyhock

Hollyhock ficifolia, commonly known as Fig-leaved Hollyhock, is a hardy perennial species valued for its long bloom period and disease resistance compared to common hollyhocks.

Description

This species produces tall, upright stems with soft, fig-shaped leaves and elegant spikes of flowers in shades of yellow, apricot, pink, and cream.

Etymology

“Ficifolia” means “fig-leaved,” referring to the distinctive shape of its foliage resembling fig tree leaves.

Taxonomy

Family: Malvaceae
Genus: Alcea
Species: A. ficifolia

Distribution and Habitat

Native to Siberia and parts of central Asia, it is cultivated widely in temperate ornamental gardens.

Ecology

Attracts bees and butterflies and is valued for supporting pollinators over a long flowering season.

Cultivation

  • Full sun preferred
  • Well-drained soil
  • More drought tolerant than A. rosea
  • Low maintenance perennial

Cultivars

  • Pastel mixed flower strains
  • Apricot and yellow selections

Symbolism

Represents endurance and natural elegance due to its long-lasting and hardy growth habit.
