Alcea pallida – Pale Hollyhock

Hollyhock pallida, commonly known as Pale Hollyhock, is a delicate perennial species valued for its soft-colored blooms and elegant garden presence.

Description

This species produces tall stems with pale cream to light pink flowers that have a subtle, refined appearance compared to more vivid hollyhocks.

Etymology

“Pallida” means “pale” in Latin, referring to the light coloration of its flowers.

Taxonomy

Family: Malvaceae
Genus: Alcea
Species: A. pallida

Distribution and Habitat

Native to parts of Central Asia and surrounding regions, it grows in open sunny meadows and steppe-like habitats.

Ecology

Provides nectar for bees and other pollinators while blending into natural meadow ecosystems.

Cultivation

  • Full sun preferred
  • Well-drained soil
  • Moderate water needs
  • Hardy in temperate climates

Cultivars

  • Wild pale forms
  • Soft pastel garden selections

Symbolism

Represents subtle beauty, grace, and quiet elegance.
