Alcea rosea – Common Hollyhock

Hollyhock rosea, commonly known as Common Hollyhock, is a tall, classic garden biennial known for its towering spikes of colorful flowers.

Description

This species produces tall upright stems covered in large, showy, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white and pink to deep red, purple, and yellow.

Etymology

“Alcea” is derived from the Greek name for mallows, reflecting its family relationship, while “rosea” refers to its rose-like blossoms.

Taxonomy

Family: Malvaceae
Genus: Alcea
Species: A. rosea

Distribution and Habitat

Native to Asia and the eastern Mediterranean, it is widely cultivated in cottage gardens and naturalized in temperate regions worldwide.

Ecology

Attracts bees, butterflies, and hummingbirds, serving as an important ornamental nectar source in gardens.

Cultivation

  • Full sun preferred
  • Well-drained fertile soil
  • Often grown as a biennial or short-lived perennial
  • May require staking due to height

Cultivars

  • ‘Chater’s Double’ – fully double blooms
  • ‘Nigra’ – deep maroon-black flowers
  • Mixed heritage cottage garden varieties

Symbolism

Represents ambition, fertility, and the passage of time due to its tall growth and dramatic flowering display.
