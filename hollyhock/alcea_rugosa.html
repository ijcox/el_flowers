Alcea rugosa – Russian Hollyhock

Hollyhock rugosa, commonly known as Russian Hollyhock, is a hardy perennial species known for its resilience and pale yellow flowers.

Description

This species produces tall stems with slightly wrinkled (rugose) leaves and soft yellow, cup-shaped flowers arranged along the spike.

Etymology

“Rugosa” means “wrinkled,” referring to the texture of its leaves.

Taxonomy

Family: Malvaceae
Genus: Alcea
Species: A. rugosa

Distribution and Habitat

Native to Russia and parts of Eastern Europe, it grows in meadows, steppe regions, and open grasslands.

Ecology

Provides nectar for bees and other insects, especially in open meadow ecosystems.

Cultivation

  • Full sun preferred
  • Well-drained soil
  • Highly cold tolerant
  • More drought tolerant than many hollyhocks

Cultivars

  • Wild pale-yellow form
  • Selected garden hardy strains

Symbolism

Represents resilience and endurance in harsh climates.
