Tiger Lily

Tiger lilies are striking ornamental flowers known for their bright orange petals covered in dark spots.

Description

Tiger lily blooms are recurved with prominent dark spots and long stamens, giving them a dramatic appearance. They grow on tall stems and often produce multiple flowers per stalk.

They are widely grown in gardens for their bold visual impact.

Taxonomy

Tiger lilies belong to the species Lilium lancifolium in the family Liliaceae.

Species

Lilium Lancifolium – Tiger Lily
Lilium Bulbiferum – Orange Lily
Lilium Candidum – Madonna Lily

Etymology

The name refers to the flower’s tiger-like spotted appearance.

Ecology

Tiger lilies attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies and can spread through bulbils in leaf axils.

Diseases and pests

  • Lily beetles feeding on leaves
  • Aphids on stems and buds
  • Fungal leaf spots
  • Bulb rot in wet soil

Cultivation

Tiger lilies are hardy perennials grown in borders and cottage gardens.

  • Light: Full sun to partial shade
  • Soil: Well-drained soil
  • Water: Moderate
  • Planting: Bulbs in autumn or spring
  • Maintenance: Minimal care required

Tiger Lily in culture

Tiger lilies symbolize pride, prosperity, and confidence in various cultures.
