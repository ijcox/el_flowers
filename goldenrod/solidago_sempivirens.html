Solidago sempervirens – Seaside Goldenrod

Goldenrod sempervirens, commonly known as Seaside Goldenrod, is a salt-tolerant perennial species found along coastal regions.

Description

This species produces thick, upright stems with dense clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer through fall.

Etymology

“Sempervirens” means “always green,” referring to its evergreen or semi-evergreen foliage in mild climates.

Taxonomy

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Solidago
Species: S. sempervirens

Distribution and Habitat

Native to coastal North America, it thrives in salt marshes, dunes, and sandy shorelines.

Ecology

An important late-season nectar source for pollinators, especially in coastal ecosystems where floral diversity is lower.

Cultivation

  • Full sun required
  • Tolerates salty soils and coastal winds
  • Prefers sandy, well-drained soil
  • Low maintenance and drought tolerant

Cultivars

  • Wild coastal form
  • Selected compact garden varieties

Symbolism

Represents endurance and adaptability due to its ability to thrive in harsh coastal environments.
