Solidago nemoralis – Gray Goldenrod

Goldenrod nemoralis, commonly known as Gray Goldenrod, is a compact perennial species known for its greyish foliage and bright golden flower clusters.

Description

This species produces shorter stems than other goldenrods, topped with dense, slightly arching clusters of small yellow flowers.

Etymology

“Nemoralis” means “of the groves or woods,” referring to its natural woodland-edge habitats.

Taxonomy

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Solidago
Species: S. nemoralis

Distribution and Habitat

Native to North America, it is commonly found in dry prairies, rocky soils, open woods, and roadsides.

Ecology

Provides nectar and pollen for bees, butterflies, and other insects during late summer and early fall.

Cultivation

  • Full sun preferred
  • Well-drained, dry to average soil
  • Drought tolerant once established
  • Compact growth habit suitable for gardens

Cultivars

  • Wild gray-green form
  • Dwarf ornamental selections

Symbolism

Represents resilience and quiet strength, thriving in dry and challenging environments.
