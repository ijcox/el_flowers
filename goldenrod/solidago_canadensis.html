Solidago canadensis – Canada Goldenrod

Goldenrod canadensis, commonly known as Canada Goldenrod, is a vigorous perennial wildflower known for its tall plumes of bright yellow flowers that light up late summer landscapes.

Description

This species produces tall, upright stems topped with dense, arching clusters of tiny golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Etymology

“Solidago” comes from Latin meaning “to make whole,” referring to historical medicinal uses. “Canadensis” indicates its strong presence in Canada and North America.

Taxonomy

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Solidago
Species: S. canadensis

Distribution and Habitat

Native to North America, it thrives in meadows, prairies, roadsides, and disturbed soils where it spreads readily.

Ecology

An important late-season nectar source for bees, butterflies, and other pollinators, supporting ecosystems as other flowers fade.

Cultivation

  • Full sun preferred
  • Tolerates poor to average soils
  • Drought tolerant once established
  • Can spread aggressively in gardens

Cultivars

  • Wild type – tall natural form
  • Compact garden selections – reduced spread

Symbolism

Represents encouragement, good fortune, and resilience due to its ability to thrive in many environments.
