Solidago odora – Sweet Goldenrod

Goldenrod odora, commonly known as Sweet Goldenrod, is a fragrant perennial species valued for its aromatic leaves and golden flower clusters.

Description

This species produces upright stems with narrow leaves and arching clusters of small yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

Etymology

“Odora” refers to the plant’s distinct sweet fragrance, especially noticeable when the leaves are crushed.

Taxonomy

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Solidago
Species: S. odora

Distribution and Habitat

Native to eastern North America, it grows in dry woods, sandy soils, and coastal plains.

Ecology

Attracts bees, butterflies, and other pollinators, and is especially valuable as a late-season nectar source.

Cultivation

  • Full sun to light shade
  • Well-drained sandy or loamy soil
  • Drought tolerant once established
  • Low maintenance perennial

Cultivars

  • Wild aromatic form
  • Selected garden strains with stronger fragrance

Symbolism

Represents healing, warmth, and natural resilience due to its fragrance and hardy growth.
