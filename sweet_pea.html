Sweet Pea

Sweet peas are fragrant climbing flowers admired for their delicate petals and strong, sweet scent.

Description

Sweet pea flowers grow in clusters on climbing vines and come in soft pastel shades as well as bold reds and purples. They are known for their ruffled, butterfly-like blooms.

They are widely used in ornamental gardens and cut flower arrangements.

Taxonomy

Sweet peas belong to the species Lathyrus odoratus in the family Fabaceae. They are native to the Mediterranean region.

Species

Lathyrus Odoratus – Sweet Pea
Lathyrus Latifolius – Perennial Sweet Pea
Lathyrus Sylvestris – Narrow-leaved Sweet Pea

Etymology

The name Lathyrus comes from Greek, while “odoratus” refers to its strong fragrance.

Ecology

Sweet peas attract bees and other pollinators and thrive in cool, temperate climates.

Diseases and pests

  • Aphids on stems and buds
  • Powdery mildew in humid conditions
  • Slugs damaging young shoots
  • Root rot in poorly drained soil

Cultivation

Sweet peas are popular climbing garden plants grown for their fragrance and ornamental value.

  • Light: Full sun
  • Soil: Fertile, well-drained soil
  • Water: Moderate
  • Planting: Seeds sown in cool seasons
  • Maintenance: Provide support for climbing vines

Sweet Pea in culture

Sweet peas symbolize pleasure, gratitude, and delicate beauty and are often used in bouquets and celebrations.
