Cosmos caudatus – Ulam Raja

Cosmos caudatus is an edible and medicinal species widely used as a leafy vegetable in Southeast Asia.

Description

A fast-growing annual with small pink to purple flowers and aromatic, finely divided leaves commonly harvested for food.

Etymology

“Caudatus” refers to a “tailed” feature in plant morphology, though commonly associated with its elongated floral structures.

Taxonomy

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Cosmos
Species: C. caudatus

Distribution and Habitat

Native to Central America, now widely naturalized and cultivated across tropical Asia.

Ecology

Provides nectar for insects and grows easily in disturbed soils and gardens.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Fast-growing and heat tolerant
  • Often grown as a vegetable crop

Cultivars

  • Mainly wild or traditional food varieties

Symbolism

Represents nourishment, vitality, and everyday resilience.
