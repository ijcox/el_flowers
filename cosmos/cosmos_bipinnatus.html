Cosmos bipinnatus – Garden Cosmos

Cosmos bipinnatus is a widely grown ornamental annual known for its airy foliage and delicate daisy-like flowers.

Description

A tall, branching annual producing pink, white, or magenta flowers with yellow centers and finely divided, feathery leaves.

Etymology

“Cosmos” comes from Greek meaning “order” or “harmony,” referring to the balanced form of its petals.

Taxonomy

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Cosmos
Species: C. bipinnatus

Distribution and Habitat

Native to Mexico and Central America, now widely cultivated in gardens around the world.

Ecology

Attracts bees, butterflies, and other pollinators throughout the growing season.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Drought tolerant
  • Self-seeds easily in gardens

Cultivars

  • ‘Sensation’ series – large, colorful blooms
  • ‘Sonata’ series – compact garden form

Symbolism

Represents harmony, peace, and natural beauty.
