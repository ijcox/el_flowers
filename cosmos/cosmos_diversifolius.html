Cosmos diversifolius – Wild Cosmos

Cosmos diversifolius is a lesser-known wild species valued for its adaptability and natural variation in leaf form.

Description

A perennial or annual herb producing small daisy-like flowers with variable leaf shapes, often finely divided or lobed.

Etymology

“Diversifolius” means “diverse leaves,” referring to its variable foliage structure.

Taxonomy

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Cosmos
Species: C. diversifolius

Distribution and Habitat

Native to Mexico and parts of Central America, growing in grasslands and open disturbed areas.

Ecology

Supports native pollinators and adapts well to varied environmental conditions.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Moderate drought tolerance
  • Mainly grown in botanical collections

Cultivars

  • No widely established cultivars

Symbolism

Represents adaptability, variation, and natural diversity.
