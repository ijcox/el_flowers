Cosmos atrosanguineus – Chocolate Cosmos

Cosmos atrosanguineus is a rare ornamental species known for its deep maroon flowers and distinctive chocolate-like scent.

Description

A tuberous perennial producing velvety dark red to almost black flowers with a rich fragrance reminiscent of chocolate or cocoa.

Etymology

“Atrosanguineus” means “dark blood-colored,” referring to its deep reddish-brown petals.

Taxonomy

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Cosmos
Species: C. atrosanguineus

Distribution and Habitat

Native to Mexico, now extinct in the wild but maintained through cultivation.

Ecology

Historically pollinated by insects, but current cultivated forms are mostly propagated vegetatively.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Grown from tubers
  • Frost tender perennial

Cultivars

  • ‘Black Magic’ – deep chocolate-red blooms
  • Improved fragrance-selected forms

Symbolism

Represents rarity, mystery, and luxury.
