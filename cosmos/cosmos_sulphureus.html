Cosmos sulphureus – Yellow Cosmos

Cosmos sulphureus is a bright, heat-tolerant flowering plant known for its vivid yellow to orange blooms.

Description

An upright annual producing golden yellow, orange, or scarlet daisy-like flowers with finely divided foliage.

Etymology

“Sulphureus” refers to sulfur-yellow coloration of the flowers.

Taxonomy

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Cosmos
Species: C. sulphureus

Distribution and Habitat

Native to Mexico, Central America, and northern South America, thriving in warm, sunny environments.

Ecology

Attracts bees, butterflies, and other pollinators, especially in hot and dry conditions where fewer plants thrive.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Very heat and drought tolerant
  • Easy to grow from seed

Cultivars

  • ‘Bright Lights’ mix – mixed orange and yellow forms
  • Compact dwarf garden varieties

Symbolism

Represents warmth, energy, and vitality.
