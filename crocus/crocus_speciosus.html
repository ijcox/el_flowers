Crocus speciosus – Autumn Crocus

Crocus speciosus is a striking autumn-flowering species known for its vibrant lilac blooms that appear as most spring flowers fade.

Description

A perennial corm producing star-shaped lilac to violet flowers with orange-red stigmas, blooming in early to mid-autumn.

Etymology

“Speciosus” means “showy” or “splendid,” referring to its attractive autumn flowers.

Taxonomy

Family: Iridaceae
Genus: Crocus
Species: C. speciosus

Distribution and Habitat

Native to the Balkans, Turkey, and parts of the Caucasus, typically found in woodland edges and rocky slopes.

Ecology

Provides late-season nectar for pollinators preparing for winter dormancy.

Cultivation

  • Full sun to partial shade
  • Well-drained soil
  • Best planted in late summer or early autumn
  • Naturalizes well in grass and woodland gardens

Cultivars

  • ‘Albus’ – white-flowered form
  • Deep violet garden selections

Symbolism

Represents transition, reflection, and beauty in seasonal change.
