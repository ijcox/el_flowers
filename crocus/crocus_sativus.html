Crocus sativus – Saffron Crocus

Crocus sativus is the famous cultivated species used to produce saffron, one of the world’s most valuable spices.

Description

A perennial flowering plant producing delicate lilac to purple flowers with vivid red stigmas, which are harvested as saffron threads.

Etymology

“Sativus” means “cultivated,” reflecting its long history of human cultivation.

Taxonomy

Family: Iridaceae
Genus: Crocus
Species: C. sativus

Distribution and Habitat

Originally derived from the eastern Mediterranean region, now cultivated widely in Iran, India, Spain, and other dry climates.

Ecology

Sterile triploid plant propagated by corms, relying entirely on human cultivation for reproduction.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Well-drained, dry soil
  • Harvested in autumn flowering season
  • Corms replanted annually in many regions

Cultivars

  • Traditional saffron-producing landraces

Symbolism

Represents luxury, rarity, and cultural heritage.
