Crocus chrysanthus – Snow Crocus

Crocus chrysanthus is an early-flowering species known for its small, bright blooms that appear very early in spring.

Description

A compact perennial producing small cup-shaped flowers in yellow, cream, and purple shades, often with delicate striping or markings.

Etymology

“Chrysanthus” means “golden flower,” referring to its frequent yellow blooms.

Taxonomy

Family: Iridaceae
Genus: Crocus
Species: C. chrysanthus

Distribution and Habitat

Native to the Balkans and surrounding regions, growing in alpine meadows and rocky grasslands.

Ecology

Important early nectar source for bees emerging after winter.

Cultivation

  • Full sun to partial shade
  • Well-drained soil
  • Naturalizes easily in lawns and rock gardens
  • Plant corms in autumn

Cultivars

  • ‘Cream Beauty’ – soft yellow form
  • ‘Advance’ – purple and yellow bicolour

Symbolism

Represents early hope, brightness, and the first signs of spring.
