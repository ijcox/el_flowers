Crocus tommasinianus – Woodland Crocus

Crocus tommasinianus is a delicate early spring species known for its naturalizing ability in woodland and grassy areas.

Description

A small perennial producing pale lilac to lavender flowers with slender petals that open widely in sunlight.

Etymology

Named after botanist Muzio Tommasini, who studied the flora of the Adriatic region.

Taxonomy

Family: Iridaceae
Genus: Crocus
Species: C. tommasinianus

Distribution and Habitat

Native to the Balkans and Central Europe, often found in deciduous woodland edges and meadows.

Ecology

Provides one of the earliest nectar sources for bees and other pollinators in late winter and early spring.

Cultivation

  • Full sun to partial shade
  • Well-drained soil
  • Excellent for naturalizing in lawns
  • Very hardy and low maintenance

Cultivars

  • ‘Ruby Giant’ – deeper purple selection
  • ‘Barr’s Purple’ – rich violet flowers

Symbolism

Represents resilience, renewal, and quiet beauty.
