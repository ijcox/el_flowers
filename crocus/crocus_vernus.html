Crocus vernus – Spring Crocus

Crocus vernus is a popular early spring-flowering species known for its vibrant blooms emerging through late winter snow.

Description

A low-growing perennial producing cup-shaped flowers in shades of purple, white, and striped forms, often opening in early spring sunlight.

Etymology

“Vernus” means “spring,” referring to its early seasonal blooming period.

Taxonomy

Family: Iridaceae
Genus: Crocus
Species: C. vernus

Distribution and Habitat

Native to alpine regions of Europe, especially the Alps and Balkans, often found in meadows and grassy slopes.

Ecology

One of the earliest nectar sources for bees emerging from winter dormancy.

Cultivation

  • Full sun to partial shade
  • Well-drained soil
  • Naturalizes well in lawns and meadows
  • Plant corms in autumn

Cultivars

  • ‘Remembrance’ – deep purple form
  • ‘Jeanne d’Arc’ – white flowering selection

Symbolism

Represents renewal, hope, and the arrival of spring.
