Gardenia jasminoides – Common Gardenia

Gardenia jasminoides is a highly fragrant evergreen shrub prized for its creamy white flowers and glossy foliage.

Description

A compact shrub producing waxy, white, rose-like flowers with an intense sweet fragrance.

Etymology

“Jasminoides” means “jasmine-like,” referring to its fragrant flowers.

Taxonomy

Family: Rubiaceae
Genus: Gardenia
Species: G. jasminoides

Distribution and Habitat

Native to East Asia, including China and Japan, typically found in warm, humid environments.

Ecology

Pollinated by insects attracted to its strong fragrance.

Cultivation

  • Partial shade to full sun
  • Acidic, well-drained soil
  • High humidity preferred
  • Sensitive to cold

Cultivars

  • ‘August Beauty’ – large fragrant blooms
  • ‘Radicans’ – low-growing form

Symbolism

Represents purity, love, and refinement.
