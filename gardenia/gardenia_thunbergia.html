Gardenia thunbergia – Forest Gardenia

A large, tropical gardenia species known for its striking white flowers and woody growth habit.

Description

A shrub or small tree producing large, star-shaped white flowers with a strong fragrance and thick, glossy leaves.

Etymology

Named in honor of Carl Peter Thunberg, a Swedish botanist and student of Linnaeus.

Taxonomy

Family: Rubiaceae
Genus: Gardenia
Species: G. thunbergia

Distribution and Habitat

Native to southern Africa, typically found in forest margins and coastal woodlands.

Ecology

Pollinated by insects and adapted to warm, humid environments.

Cultivation

  • Full sun to partial shade
  • Well-drained soil
  • Warm climates preferred
  • Can grow into a small tree

Cultivars

  • Mostly species form in cultivation

Symbolism

Represents strength, elegance, and tropical beauty.
