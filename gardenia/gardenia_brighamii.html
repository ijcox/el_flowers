Gardenia brighamii – Hawaiian Gardenia

A rare and endangered species of gardenia native to Hawaii, known for its fragrant white flowers.

Description

A small tree or shrub producing creamy white, tubular flowers with a strong, pleasant fragrance.

Etymology

Named after William Tufts Brigham, an American botanist and naturalist.

Taxonomy

Family: Rubiaceae
Genus: Gardenia
Species: G. brighamii

Distribution and Habitat

Endemic to Hawaii, found in dry forests and shrublands, though now extremely limited in the wild.

Ecology

Pollination ecology is not fully understood; possibly pollinated by native insects or birds.

Cultivation

  • Warm, frost-free climates
  • Well-drained soil
  • Full sun to partial shade
  • Grown in conservation programs

Cultivars

  • No major cultivars; conservation-focused propagation

Symbolism

Represents rarity, conservation, and island heritage.
