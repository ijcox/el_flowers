Gardenia carinata – Yellow-veined Gardenia

Gardenia carinata is a tropical species known for its distinctive yellow-veined flowers and ornamental foliage.

Description

A shrub or small tree producing creamy white to pale yellow flowers, often with subtle veining, and glossy green leaves.

Etymology

“Carinata” refers to a “keeled” or ridged structure, often describing features of the plant’s floral parts.

Taxonomy

Family: Rubiaceae
Genus: Gardenia
Species: G. carinata

Distribution and Habitat

Native to Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia, where it grows in tropical forests.

Ecology

Pollinated by insects and adapted to warm, humid environments.

Cultivation

  • Warm tropical climates
  • Partial shade
  • Moist, well-drained soil
  • Frost-sensitive species

Cultivars

  • Rare in cultivation; mainly grown in botanical collections

Symbolism

Represents exotic beauty, uniqueness, and tropical elegance.
