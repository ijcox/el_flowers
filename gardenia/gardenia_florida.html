Gardenia florida – Wild Cape Jasmine

Gardenia florida is an older botanical name often associated with wild or natural forms of Cape jasmine-type gardenias.

Description

An evergreen shrub producing fragrant white flowers and glossy dark green leaves, similar to cultivated gardenias.

Etymology

“Florida” refers to “flowery” rather than the U.S. state, indicating its abundant blooms.

Taxonomy

Family: Rubiaceae
Genus: Gardenia
Species: G. florida (often considered synonymous with G. jasminoides)

Distribution and Habitat

Native to East Asia, found in subtropical forests and warm, humid regions.

Ecology

Attracts pollinating insects with its strong fragrance and nectar.

Cultivation

  • Partial shade
  • Acidic, well-drained soil
  • High humidity preferred
  • Sensitive to cold and drought

Cultivars

  • Forms overlap with cultivated gardenia varieties

Symbolism

Represents purity, grace, and timeless beauty.
