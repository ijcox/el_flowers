Wallflower

Wallflowers are hardy flowering plants known for their warm-colored blooms and ability to grow in rocky or dry conditions.

Description

Wallflower blossoms appear in clusters of yellow, orange, red, or purple tones and often have a pleasant fragrance.

They are commonly found growing on walls, cliffs, and rocky slopes.

Taxonomy

Wallflowers belong to the genus Erysimum in the family Brassicaceae.

Species

Erysimum Cheiri – Common Wallflower
Erysimum Bowles's Mauve – Perennial Wallflower
Erysimum Capitatum – Western Wallflower

Etymology

The name “wallflower” comes from its tendency to grow on old stone walls and rocky surfaces.

Ecology

Wallflowers provide nectar for bees and butterflies and thrive in poor, dry soils.

Diseases and pests

  • Aphids on stems and buds
  • Downy mildew in wet conditions
  • Flea beetles damaging leaves
  • Root rot in poorly drained soil

Cultivation

Wallflowers are popular in cottage gardens and rock gardens for their color and fragrance.

  • Light: Full sun
  • Soil: Well-drained, poor to moderate soil
  • Water: Low to moderate
  • Planting: Seeds or cuttings
  • Maintenance: Deadhead for prolonged bloom

Wallflower in culture

Wallflowers symbolize faithfulness in adversity and quiet resilience.
