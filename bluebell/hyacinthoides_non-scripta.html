Hyacinthoides non-scripta – English Bluebell

Bluebell non-scripta is a classic woodland wildflower famous for its dense carpets of blue in spring forests.

Description

Produces nodding, tubular deep-blue flowers with a strong sweet fragrance and arching stems.

Etymology

“Non-scripta” means “unwritten,” distinguishing it from related species with marked petals.

Taxonomy

Family: Asparagaceae
Genus: Hyacinthoides
Species: H. non-scripta

Distribution and Habitat

Native to western Europe, especially the British Isles, thriving in ancient woodlands.

Ecology

Important early nectar source for pollinators; forms dense colonies via bulbs.

Cultivation

  • Partial shade
  • Moist, well-drained soil
  • Naturalises well in woodland gardens

Cultivars

  • Mostly wild-type in cultivation

Symbolism

Represents gratitude, humility, and everlasting love.
