Mertensia virginica – Virginia Bluebell

Bluebell virginica is a spring ephemeral woodland plant native to eastern North America, known for its soft blue bell-shaped flowers.

Description

Produces clusters of nodding, trumpet-shaped flowers that begin pink and mature to sky blue.

Etymology

“Virginica” refers to Virginia, part of its native range.

Taxonomy

Family: Boraginaceae
Genus: Mertensia
Species: M. virginica

Distribution and Habitat

Native to moist deciduous woodlands and floodplains of eastern North America.

Ecology

Early spring bloomer providing nectar for bees before canopy leaf-out.

Cultivation

  • Partial shade
  • Rich, moist soil
  • Dormant after spring flowering

Cultivars

  • Primarily wild type in cultivation

Symbolism

Represents renewal, fleeting beauty, and the arrival of spring.
