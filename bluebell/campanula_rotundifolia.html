Campanula rotundifolia – Harebell

Bellflower rotundifolia is a delicate wildflower known for its nodding blue bells and airy appearance in grasslands and rocky habitats.

Description

Slender stems bearing small, nodding, bell-shaped blue flowers with narrow basal leaves that often wither by flowering time.

Etymology

“Rotundifolia” means “round-leaved,” referring to the early basal leaves.

Taxonomy

Family: Campanulaceae
Genus: Campanula
Species: C. rotundifolia

Distribution and Habitat

Widespread across Europe, North America, and parts of Asia in dry grasslands, dunes, and rocky soils.

Ecology

Important nectar source for bees and butterflies in poor soils and open habitats.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Well-drained, sandy or rocky soil
  • Drought tolerant

Cultivars

  • Mostly wild-type; few ornamental selections

Symbolism

Represents humility, resilience, and delicate natural beauty.
