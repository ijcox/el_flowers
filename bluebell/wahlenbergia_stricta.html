Wahlenbergia stricta – Australian Bluebell

Bluebell stricta is a native Australian wildflower known for its delicate starry blue blooms.

Description

Produces slender stems with pale to deep blue, five-petaled bell-shaped flowers.

Etymology

Named after Swedish botanist Göran Wahlenberg; “stricta” means upright or straight.

Taxonomy

Family: Campanulaceae
Genus: Wahlenbergia
Species: W. stricta

Distribution and Habitat

Native to Australia, found in grasslands, open forests, and rocky slopes.

Ecology

Provides nectar for native bees and insects, especially in dry habitats.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Drought tolerant once established

Cultivars

  • Mainly wild form in cultivation

Symbolism

Represents endurance, simplicity, and native resilience.
