Hyacinthoides hispanica – Spanish Bluebell

Bluebell hispanica is a robust garden species often grown for its upright flowers and tolerance of varied conditions.

Description

Produces upright clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, pink, or white with broader leaves than English bluebell.

Etymology

“Hispanica” refers to its native range in the Iberian Peninsula.

Taxonomy

Family: Asparagaceae
Genus: Hyacinthoides
Species: H. hispanica

Distribution and Habitat

Native to Spain and Portugal, commonly found in open woodlands and meadows.

Ecology

Readily hybridises with English bluebell in overlapping regions, affecting native populations.

Cultivation

  • Full sun to partial shade
  • Adaptable soil types
  • Very hardy and vigorous grower

Cultivars

  • ‘Excelsior’
  • Pink and white garden forms

Symbolism

Represents resilience, adaptability, and spring renewal.
