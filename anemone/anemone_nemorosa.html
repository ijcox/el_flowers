Anemone Nemorosa – Wood Anemone

Anemone Nemorosa is a delicate woodland wildflower that forms dense spring carpets of white blooms across forest floors.

Description

A low-growing perennial with finely divided leaves and star-shaped white flowers, often tinged with pink or purple on the underside.

Etymology

“Nemorosa” comes from Latin meaning “of the woods,” referring to its natural woodland habitat.

Taxonomy

Family: Ranunculaceae
Genus: Anemone
Species: A. nemorosa

Distribution and Habitat

Native to Europe and parts of western Asia, typically found in deciduous woodlands and shaded forest floors.

Ecology

An early spring ephemerals species that blooms before tree canopies fully develop, supporting early pollinators.

Cultivation

  • Partial to full shade
  • Humus-rich, moist soil
  • Spreads slowly via rhizomes
  • Best planted under deciduous trees

Cultivars

  • ‘Robinsoniana’
  • ‘Allenii’

Symbolism

Represents purity, transience, and the quiet beauty of spring forests.
