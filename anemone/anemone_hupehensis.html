Anemone Hupehensis – Japanese Anemone Group

Anemone Hupehensis is a graceful late-summer flowering perennial known for its tall stems and soft pink or white blooms.

Description

Produces elegant cup-shaped flowers on tall branching stems. Blooms appear in late summer to autumn and sway gently in the wind.

Etymology

Named after Hupeh (Hubei), China, where it was first documented by botanists.

Taxonomy

Family: Ranunculaceae
Genus: Anemone
Species group: hupehensis

Distribution and Habitat

Native to China but widely cultivated and naturalized in Japan, Europe, and North America. Prefers woodland edges and shaded slopes.

Ecology

Provides important late-season nectar for bees and other pollinators when few flowers remain in bloom.

Cultivation

  • Partial shade
  • Moist, fertile, well-drained soil
  • Mulching recommended in winter
  • Spreads gradually via rhizomes

Cultivars

  • ‘Honorine Jobert’
  • ‘September Charm’
  • ‘Pamina’

Symbolism

Represents endurance, anticipation, and quiet beauty in Japanese and Western garden traditions.
