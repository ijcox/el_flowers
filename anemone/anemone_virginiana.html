Anemone Virginiana – Tall Thimbleweed

Anemone Virginiana is a tall native North American wildflower known for its distinctive seed heads resembling small thimbles.

Description

Produces upright stems with small white flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical seed heads. Each seed head is dry and thimble-shaped.

Etymology

“Virginiana” refers to Virginia, one of its early recorded native regions.

Taxonomy

Family: Ranunculaceae
Genus: Anemone
Species: A. virginiana

Distribution and Habitat

Native to eastern and central North America. Found in dry open woods, prairies, and meadow edges.

Ecology

Supports native pollinators and provides seeds for small wildlife species. Adapted to dry and open habitats.

Cultivation

  • Full sun to light shade
  • Well-drained to dry soil
  • Drought tolerant once established
  • Spreads slowly in gardens

Cultivars

  • Mainly wild-type species used

Symbolism

Represents resilience, wild beauty, and natural endurance in native landscapes.
