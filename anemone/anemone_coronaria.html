Anemone Coronaria – Poppy Anemone

Anemone Coronaria is a vibrant Mediterranean flowering plant known for its bold, poppy-like blooms and striking color range.

Description

A tuberous perennial producing large, showy flowers in red, blue, white, and purple. Each bloom typically features a dark central eye surrounded by delicate petals.

Etymology

“Anemone” comes from Greek meaning “wind flower,” while “coronaria” refers to its historical use in floral garlands and crowns.

Taxonomy

Family: Ranunculaceae
Genus: Anemone
Species: A. coronaria

Distribution and Habitat

Native to the Mediterranean basin, including southern Europe, North Africa, and the Middle East. Typically found in rocky slopes and open grasslands.

Ecology

Pollinated by bees and beetles. The plant survives dry summers as underground tubers, re-sprouting in cooler seasons.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Well-drained sandy or loamy soil
  • Moderate watering during growth
  • Autumn planting of tubers

Cultivars

  • De Caen Group
  • St. Brigid Group

Symbolism

Represents anticipation, protection, and fragile beauty in Mediterranean floral traditions.
