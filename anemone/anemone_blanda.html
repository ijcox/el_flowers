Anemone Blanda – Grecian Windflower

Anemone Blanda is a low-growing spring-flowering plant known for forming colorful ground-cover carpets.

Description

Produces daisy-like flowers in shades of blue, pink, white, and violet. Each bloom opens in sunlight and closes at night.

Etymology

“Blanda” comes from Latin meaning gentle or charming, referring to its soft appearance.

Taxonomy

Family: Ranunculaceae
Genus: Anemone
Species: A. blanda

Distribution and Habitat

Native to southeastern Europe, including Greece, Turkey, and the Balkans. Found in rocky slopes and woodland edges.

Ecology

Early spring nectar source for bees and small pollinators emerging after winter.

Cultivation

  • Full sun to partial shade
  • Well-drained soil
  • Planted as tubers in autumn
  • Naturalizes into carpets over time

Cultivars

  • ‘Blue Shades’
  • ‘White Splendour’
  • ‘Charmer’

Symbolism

Represents anticipation, renewal, and gentle beauty in spring gardens.
