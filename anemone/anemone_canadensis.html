Anemone Canadensis – Canada Anemone

Anemone Canadensis is a hardy North American wildflower that spreads to form attractive ground cover in moist habitats.

Description

Produces white, star-shaped flowers with yellow centers. Stems are slender and often reach up to 60 cm tall.

Etymology

“Canadensis” refers to Canada, one of its primary native regions.

Taxonomy

Family: Ranunculaceae
Genus: Anemone
Species: A. canadensis

Distribution and Habitat

Native to North America, especially Canada and the northern United States. Found in meadows, riverbanks, and moist prairies.

Ecology

Provides nectar for bees and butterflies and helps stabilize soil in riparian environments.

Cultivation

  • Full sun to partial shade
  • Moist, well-drained soil
  • Spreads aggressively via rhizomes
  • Useful as ground cover

Cultivars

  • Primarily wild-type cultivation

Symbolism

Represents resilience, natural strength, and adaptability.
