Lotus

Lotus is a sacred aquatic flowering plant known for its striking blooms and symbolic importance in many cultures.

Description

Lotus flowers rise above water on long stems and feature large, layered petals in shades of pink, white, or yellow. The plant has broad circular leaves that float or rise above the water surface.

It is often confused with water lilies but differs in structure and growth habit.

Taxonomy

Lotus belongs to the genus Nelumbo in the family Nelumbonaceae. It is native to Asia and parts of Australia.

Species

Nelumbo Nucifera – Sacred Lotus
Nelumbo Lutea – American Lotus

Etymology

The name Nelumbo comes from Sri Lankan Tamil “nelum,” meaning lotus flower.

Ecology

Lotus grows in ponds, lakes, and slow-moving waters and plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems.

Diseases and pests

  • Aphids on leaves and buds
  • Fungal leaf spot diseases
  • Root rot in stagnant water
  • Caterpillar feeding damage

Cultivation

Lotus is widely cultivated in ornamental ponds and water gardens for its beauty and cultural significance.

  • Light: Full sun
  • Water: Shallow, still water
  • Soil: Heavy clay or pond soil
  • Planting: Rhizomes in submerged containers
  • Maintenance: Remove dead foliage regularly

Lotus in culture

Lotus symbolizes purity, enlightenment, and rebirth and is highly significant in Buddhism, Hinduism, and Egyptian mythology.
