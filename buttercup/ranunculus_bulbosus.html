Ranunculus bulbosus – Bulbous Buttercup

Buttercup bulbosus, known as bulbous buttercup, is a perennial wildflower recognizable by its swollen stem base and bright yellow flowers.

Description

A low to medium herb with a bulb-like swollen base at ground level. It produces glossy yellow flowers with five petals and deeply lobed leaves.

Etymology

“Bulbosus” refers to its bulb-like swollen stem base, a distinguishing characteristic of this species.

Taxonomy

Family: Ranunculaceae
Genus: Ranunculus
Species: R. bulbosus

Distribution and Habitat

Native to Europe and naturalized in many regions worldwide, commonly found in dry grasslands, meadows, and roadsides.

Ecology

Grows in nutrient-poor soils and is well adapted to grazing environments due to its bitter, toxic compounds that deter herbivores.

Cultivation

  • Prefers dry to moderately moist soils
  • Full sun exposure
  • Often considered a wildflower or weed
  • Hardy and low maintenance

Cultivars

  • Rarely cultivated intentionally

Symbolism

Represents resilience in poor soils and natural persistence in open landscapes.
