Ranunculus asiaticus – Persian Buttercup

Buttercup asiaticus, known as Persian buttercup, is a highly ornamental species widely cultivated for its large, rose-like flowers.

Description

A tuberous-rooted perennial producing layered, rose-like blooms in a wide range of colors including red, pink, yellow, orange, and white. Flowers are large and highly decorative.

Etymology

“Asiaticus” indicates its origin in regions of western Asia.

Taxonomy

Family: Ranunculaceae
Genus: Ranunculus
Species: R. asiaticus

Distribution and Habitat

Native to the eastern Mediterranean and parts of Asia, commonly grown in gardens worldwide as a seasonal ornamental plant.

Ecology

In the wild, it grows in open, rocky habitats. In cultivation, it is valued for attracting pollinators such as bees.

Cultivation

  • Prefers cool, mild climates
  • Well-drained, sandy soil
  • Full sun to partial shade
  • Grown from tuberous roots planted seasonally

Cultivars

  • ‘Bloomingdale’ series – compact double blooms
  • ‘Tecolote’ series – large, multi-layered flowers
  • ‘Cloni’ series – premium cut-flower varieties

Symbolism

Symbolizes charm, attractiveness, and radiant beauty, often used in floral arrangements and wedding bouquets.
