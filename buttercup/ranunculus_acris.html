Ranunculus acris – Meadow Buttercup

Buttercup acris, commonly known as meadow buttercup, is a tall, bright yellow wildflower found in grasslands and meadows.

Description

An upright perennial herb reaching up to around 1 meter in height. It produces glossy, butter-yellow five-petaled flowers and deeply divided leaves.

Etymology

“Acris” means sharp or bitter, referring to the plant’s acrid, toxic compounds that make it unpalatable to grazing animals.

Taxonomy

Family: Ranunculaceae
Genus: Ranunculus
Species: R. acris

Distribution and Habitat

Native to Europe and Asia, now widely naturalized across temperate regions in meadows, pastures, and roadsides.

Ecology

Thrives in open grasslands and can become dominant in overgrazed fields due to its toxicity to livestock.

Cultivation

  • Prefers full sun
  • Grows in moist to moderately dry soils
  • Often considered a wild or weedy species
  • Hardy perennial

Cultivars

  • Rarely cultivated intentionally

Symbolism

Represents wild meadow beauty and resilience in natural grassland ecosystems.
