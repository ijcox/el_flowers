Ranunculus repens – Creeping Buttercup

Buttercup repens, commonly known as creeping buttercup, is a low-growing perennial plant often found in lawns, meadows, and damp grasslands.

Description

A spreading herb with creeping stolons that root at nodes. It produces glossy yellow five-petaled flowers and deeply divided green leaves.

Etymology

“Ranunculus” comes from Latin meaning “little frog,” referring to its preference for damp habitats. “Repens” means creeping.

Taxonomy

Family: Ranunculaceae
Genus: Ranunculus
Species: R. repens

Distribution and Habitat

Native to Europe and parts of Asia, now widespread globally in moist grasslands, gardens, and disturbed soils.

Ecology

Spreads aggressively via runners, often forming dense mats that compete with other vegetation. Flowers attract bees and other pollinators.

Cultivation

  • Thrives in moist soil
  • Prefers partial to full sun
  • Often considered a weed in managed lawns
  • Very hardy and fast spreading

Cultivars

  • Generally not cultivated intentionally due to invasive growth habit

Symbolism

Often associated with simplicity and wild growth, though in gardening contexts it is usually viewed as an unwanted but resilient plant.
