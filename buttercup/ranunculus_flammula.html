Ranunculus flammula – Lesser Spearwort

Buttercup flammula, known as lesser spearwort, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic wildflower found in wet habitats.

Description

A delicate perennial herb with slender stems and narrow leaves. It produces small, bright yellow buttercup-like flowers with five petals.

Etymology

“Flammula” means “little flame,” referring to its small but bright yellow flowers.

Taxonomy

Family: Ranunculaceae
Genus: Ranunculus
Species: R. flammula

Distribution and Habitat

Native to Europe and parts of Asia, commonly found in wetlands, pond margins, marshes, and damp grasslands.

Ecology

Well adapted to waterlogged soils and shallow water environments, providing nectar for small aquatic insects and pollinators.

Cultivation

  • Requires consistently moist or wet soil
  • Full sun to partial shade
  • Often used in natural water garden edges
  • Low growing and spreading in suitable habitats

Cultivars

  • Rarely cultivated, mostly seen in wild or naturalized settings

Symbolism

Represents subtle beauty, resilience in wet landscapes, and quiet persistence in nature.
