Ranunculus ficaria – Lesser Celandine

Buttercup ficaria, commonly known as lesser celandine, is a low-growing early spring wildflower with glossy yellow blooms.

Description

A perennial herb with heart-shaped leaves and bright, glossy yellow flowers. It often forms dense ground cover in woodland areas.

Etymology

“Ficaria” refers to the fig-like tubers found on its roots.

Taxonomy

Family: Ranunculaceae
Genus: Ranunculus
Species: R. ficaria

Distribution and Habitat

Native to Europe and western Asia, commonly found in damp woodlands, hedgerows, and riverbanks.

Ecology

One of the earliest flowering plants in spring, providing an important nectar source for early pollinators.

Cultivation

  • Prefers moist, shaded environments
  • Spreads aggressively via tubers
  • Often considered invasive in some regions
  • Low maintenance ground cover

Cultivars

  • ‘Brazen Hussy’ – dark bronze foliage variety

Symbolism

Represents early renewal, spring awakening, and natural persistence.
