Lamprocapnos spectabilis – Classic Bleeding Heart

Bleeding Heart spectabilis is a graceful perennial known for its arching stems and heart-shaped dangling flowers.

Description

Produces arching stems lined with pink heart-shaped flowers with a white “drop” beneath each bloom.

Etymology

“Spectabilis” means “showy” or “notable,” referring to its striking floral display.

Taxonomy

Family: Papaveraceae
Genus: Lamprocapnos
Species: L. spectabilis

Distribution and Habitat

Native to East Asia, especially China, Korea, and Japan, in cool forest understories.

Ecology

Pollinated by bees; blooms in spring and goes dormant in summer heat.

Cultivation

  • Partial shade
  • Cool, moist, well-drained soil
  • Dies back after flowering season

Cultivars

  • ‘Alba’ (white form)
  • ‘Gold Heart’

Symbolism

Represents love, compassion, and emotional expression.
