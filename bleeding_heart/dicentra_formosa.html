Dicentra formosa – Pacific Bleeding Heart

Bleeding Heart formosa is a native woodland perennial of western North America with delicate fern-like foliage.

Description

Produces clusters of pink to purplish heart-shaped flowers above finely divided, fern-like leaves.

Etymology

“Formosa” means “beautifully formed,” referring to its graceful structure.

Taxonomy

Family: Papaveraceae
Genus: Dicentra
Species: D. formosa

Distribution and Habitat

Native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, found in moist woodland and shaded forest floors.

Ecology

Provides nectar for early-season pollinators including bees and hummingbirds.

Cultivation

  • Partial to full shade
  • Moist, humus-rich soil
  • Spreads slowly by rhizomes

Cultivars

  • ‘Bacchanal’ (deep red form)

Symbolism

Represents gentle love, nature’s subtle beauty, and resilience in shade.
