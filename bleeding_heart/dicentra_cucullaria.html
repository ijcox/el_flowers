Dicentra cucullaria – Dutchman’s Breeches Relative

Bleeding Heart cucullaria is a spring ephemeral woodland plant known for its distinctive upside-down flower shape.

Description

Produces white, yellow-tipped flowers shaped like inverted pantaloons (“breeches”) above finely divided foliage.

Etymology

“Cucullaria” refers to hood-like structures of the flower, from Latin “cucullus” meaning hood.

Taxonomy

Family: Papaveraceae
Genus: Dicentra
Species: D. cucullaria

Distribution and Habitat

Native to eastern North America, found in deciduous forests and rich woodland soils.

Ecology

A spring ephemeral that flowers early before tree canopy closure; pollinated by early bees.

Cultivation

  • Partial shade
  • Moist, rich woodland soil
  • Dormant in summer

Cultivars

  • Mainly wild species in cultivation

Symbolism

Represents fleeting beauty and the arrival of spring.
