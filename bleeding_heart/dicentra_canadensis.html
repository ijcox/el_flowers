Dicentra canadensis – Squirrel Corn Relative

Bleeding Heart canadensis is a delicate woodland spring ephemeral closely related to squirrel corn and other Dicentra species.

Description

Produces small white heart-shaped flowers and yellow underground tubers resembling corn kernels.

Etymology

“Canadensis” refers to Canada, part of its native range.

Taxonomy

Family: Papaveraceae
Genus: Dicentra
Species: D. canadensis

Distribution and Habitat

Native to eastern North America, growing in rich deciduous forest soils alongside other spring ephemerals.

Ecology

Flowers early in spring before canopy closure; pollinated by small bees and insects.

Cultivation

  • Partial to full shade
  • Humus-rich, moist soil
  • Dormant in summer months

Cultivars

  • Rarely cultivated outside wildflower collections

Symbolism

Represents subtle beauty, hidden life cycles, and renewal.
