Dicentra eximia – Fringed Bleeding Heart

Bleeding Heart eximia is a long-blooming woodland perennial valued for its finely cut foliage and extended flowering season.

Description

Produces clusters of pink to reddish-pink heart-shaped flowers above soft, finely divided, fern-like leaves.

Etymology

“Eximia” means “distinguished” or “exceptional,” reflecting its ornamental value.

Taxonomy

Family: Papaveraceae
Genus: Dicentra
Species: D. eximia

Distribution and Habitat

Native to the Appalachian Mountains of eastern North America in shaded rocky woodlands.

Ecology

Attracts bees and other small pollinators; blooms longer than many other Bleeding Heart species.

Cultivation

  • Partial shade
  • Moist, well-drained soil
  • Tolerates summer heat better than D. spectabilis

Cultivars

  • ‘Luxuriant’
  • ‘Adrian Bloom’

Symbolism

Represents endurance in love and delicate emotional strength.
