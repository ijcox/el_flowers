Gladiolus murielae – Abyssinian Sword Lily

Gladiolus murielae, commonly known as Abyssinian Sword Lily, is a fragrant, late-summer flowering species prized for its elegant white blooms.

Description

This plant produces slender stems bearing star-shaped white flowers with a deep purple blotch in the center, emitting a sweet fragrance, especially in the evening.

Etymology

Named “murielae” in honor of Muriel Wilson, a British plant collector associated with its discovery and introduction to horticulture.

Taxonomy

Family: Iridaceae
Genus: Gladiolus
Species: G. murielae

Distribution and Habitat

Native to eastern Africa, particularly Ethiopia and surrounding regions, it grows in open grasslands and rocky slopes.

Ecology

Its evening fragrance attracts nocturnal pollinators such as moths, contributing to its ecological role in native habitats.

Cultivation

  • Full sun preferred
  • Well-drained fertile soil
  • Regular watering during growth season
  • Protect from frost in colder climates

Cultivars

  • Pure white forms
  • Improved garden hybrids with stronger fragrance

Symbolism

Represents purity, elegance, and quiet strength due to its delicate appearance and fragrance.
