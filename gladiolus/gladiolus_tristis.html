Gladiolus tristis – Night-scented Gladiolus

Gladiolus tristis, commonly known as Night-scented Gladiolus, is a fragrant flowering species known for its pale blooms and strong evening scent.

Description

This species produces slender stems with delicate pale yellow to cream flowers, often marked with faint green or brown lines. The fragrance intensifies in the evening and at night.

Etymology

The species name “tristis” means “sad” or “somber” in Latin, likely referring to its muted flower coloration compared to more vivid gladiolus species.

Taxonomy

Family: Iridaceae
Genus: Gladiolus
Species: G. tristis

Distribution and Habitat

Native to South Africa, it grows in coastal and mountainous regions, often in sandy soils and open grasslands.

Ecology

Its nocturnal fragrance attracts moths, which serve as primary pollinators, making it an important species for night-active insect ecology.

Cultivation

  • Full sun to light shade
  • Well-drained sandy soil
  • Light watering, avoid waterlogging
  • Frost-sensitive in colder climates

Cultivars

  • Wild-type pale forms
  • Selected fragrant garden strains

Symbolism

Represents mystery, elegance, and quiet beauty, often associated with evening gardens and subtle fragrance.
