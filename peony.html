Peony

Peonies are luxurious flowering plants celebrated for their large, lush blooms and long garden lifespan.

Description

Peony flowers may be single, semi-double, or fully double, appearing in shades of white, pink, red, coral, and yellow. Their petals are soft and layered, often highly fragrant.

Plants may be herbaceous perennials or woody shrubs known as tree peonies.

Taxonomy

Peonies belong to the genus Paeonia in the family Paeoniaceae. They are native to Asia, Europe, and western North America.

Species

Paeonia Lactiflora – Chinese Peony
Paeonia Suffruticosa – Tree Peony
Paeonia Officinalis – Common Peony
Paeonia Tenuifolia – Fernleaf Peony

Etymology

The name Paeonia derives from Paeon, the physician of the Greek gods.

Ecology

Peonies attract bees and beneficial insects and prefer temperate climates with winter dormancy.

Diseases and pests

  • Botrytis blight on buds and stems
  • Powdery mildew in humid weather
  • Ants on flower buds (generally harmless)
  • Root rot in soggy soil

Cultivation

Peonies are treasured ornamental plants that can thrive for decades once established.

  • Light: Full sun to partial shade
  • Soil: Rich, well-drained soil
  • Water: Moderate
  • Planting: Bare roots or divisions in autumn
  • Maintenance: Avoid moving mature plants

Peony in culture

Peonies symbolize prosperity, romance, honor, and happy marriage.
