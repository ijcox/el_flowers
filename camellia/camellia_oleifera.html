Camellia oleifera – Oil Camellia

Camellia oleifera is a species widely cultivated for its seeds, which are pressed to produce tea seed oil, a valuable cooking oil in East Asia.

Description

An evergreen shrub producing white, fragrant flowers and small oil-rich seeds contained in woody capsules.

Etymology

“Oleifera” means “oil-bearing,” referring to its seeds used in oil production.

Taxonomy

Family: Theaceae
Genus: Camellia
Species: C. oleifera

Distribution and Habitat

Native to China, commonly cultivated in southern and central regions in warm, humid climates.

Ecology

Provides nectar for pollinators, while its seeds are an important agricultural resource for oil production.

Cultivation

  • Prefers acidic, well-drained soil
  • Full sun to partial shade
  • Drought-tolerant once established
  • Grown in plantations for oil harvest

Cultivars

  • Selected varieties bred for higher oil yield
  • Cold-hardy strains for northern cultivation

Symbolism

Represents nourishment, utility, and agricultural value, reflecting its importance as a resource plant.
