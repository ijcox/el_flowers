Camellia reticulata – Large-flowered Camellia

Camellia reticulata is a striking ornamental species known for its exceptionally large, showy blooms and bold presence in gardens.

Description

An evergreen shrub or small tree producing some of the largest flowers in the camellia genus, often in shades of pink, rose, or red with prominent golden stamens.

Etymology

“Reticulata” refers to the net-like pattern of veins on its leaves.

Taxonomy

Family: Theaceae
Genus: Camellia
Species: C. reticulata

Distribution and Habitat

Native to southwestern China, typically found in forested mountainous regions with mild, humid conditions.

Ecology

Attracts pollinators such as bees. Its large flowers make it an important ornamental species rather than a dominant ecological plant.

Cultivation

  • Prefers partial shade
  • Rich, acidic, well-drained soil
  • Protected from strong winds due to large flowers
  • Slow-growing but long-lived

Cultivars

  • ‘Crimson Robe’ – deep red, very large blooms
  • ‘Lion Head’ – densely petaled flower form
  • ‘Jury’s Yellow’ – rare yellow-flushed selection

Symbolism

Represents grandeur, admiration, and refined beauty due to its large and dramatic blossoms.
