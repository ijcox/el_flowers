Camellia japonica – Common Camellia

Camellia japonica is one of the most widely grown ornamental camellias, prized for its elegant, rose-like flowers that bloom in winter and early spring.

Description

An evergreen shrub or small tree with glossy dark green leaves and large, layered flowers in shades of red, pink, and white.

Etymology

“Japonica” refers to Japan, where the plant has been cultivated for centuries as an ornamental species.

Taxonomy

Family: Theaceae
Genus: Camellia
Species: C. japonica

Distribution and Habitat

Native to Japan, China, and Korea, typically found in woodland edges and forested hillsides in mild, humid climates.

Ecology

Provides nectar for early-season pollinators. Its dense foliage also offers shelter for small birds.

Cultivation

  • Prefers partial shade
  • Acidic, well-drained soil
  • Protect from harsh winter sun and wind
  • Slow to moderate growth rate

Cultivars

  • ‘Alba Plena’ – double white flowers
  • ‘Debutante’ – soft pink blooms
  • ‘Black Lace’ – deep red, velvety petals

Symbolism

Represents refinement, longevity, and admiration, often associated with elegance in Eastern gardens.
