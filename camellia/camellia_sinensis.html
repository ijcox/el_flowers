Camellia sinensis – Tea Plant

Camellia sinensis is the species of plant from which all true tea (green, black, white, oolong, and pu-erh) is derived.

Description

An evergreen shrub or small tree with glossy leaves and small white fragrant flowers. Young leaves and buds are harvested for tea production.

Etymology

“Sinensis” means “from China,” reflecting its historical origin and early cultivation in East Asia.

Taxonomy

Family: Theaceae
Genus: Camellia
Species: C. sinensis

Distribution and Habitat

Native to China, India, and Southeast Asia, typically grown in highland plantations and subtropical climates.

Ecology

Cultivated extensively in plantations; flowers provide nectar for insects, while leaves are economically significant for global agriculture.

Cultivation

  • Prefers acidic, well-drained soil
  • Partial shade to full sun depending on climate
  • Requires regular harvesting of young leaves
  • Grown in humid, mild climates

Varieties

  • Assam variety – strong, robust flavor
  • China variety – lighter, more delicate flavor
  • Hybrid cultivars – widely used in commercial tea production

Symbolism

Represents calm, mindfulness, and cultural tradition, deeply associated with tea ceremonies worldwide.
