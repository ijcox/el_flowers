Camellia sasanqua – Autumn Camellia

Camellia sasanqua is a graceful flowering shrub known for blooming earlier than most camellias, often from autumn into early winter.

Description

An evergreen shrub with smaller, more delicate flowers than Camellia japonica. Blooms are usually white or pale pink with a light fragrance.

Etymology

“Sasanqua” is derived from the Japanese name for the plant, traditionally associated with tea gardens and hedges.

Taxonomy

Family: Theaceae
Genus: Camellia
Species: C. sasanqua

Distribution and Habitat

Native to southern Japan and China, commonly found in forest edges and coastal hills in warm, humid climates.

Ecology

Provides nectar for pollinators during cooler months when few other plants are in bloom.

Cultivation

  • Prefers partial sun to light shade
  • Well-drained, acidic soil
  • More sun-tolerant than C. japonica
  • Often used for hedges and screens

Cultivars

  • ‘Yuletide’ – red flowers with yellow stamens
  • ‘Setsugekka’ – white, slightly ruffled blooms
  • ‘Kanjiro’ – deep pink, semi-double flowers

Symbolism

Represents anticipation and quiet beauty, often associated with the arrival of autumn.
