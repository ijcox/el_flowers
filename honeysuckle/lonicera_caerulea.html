Lonicera caerulea – Blue Honeysuckle

Honeysuckle caerulea, commonly known as Blue Honeysuckle or Honeyberry, is a cold-hardy fruit-bearing shrub valued for its edible blue berries.

Description

This species produces upright shrubs with opposite leaves and small yellowish tubular flowers, followed by elongated dark blue edible berries.

Etymology

“Caerulea” means “blue,” referring to the color of its fruit.

Taxonomy

Family: Caprifoliaceae
Genus: Lonicera
Species: L. caerulea

Distribution and Habitat

Native to cool temperate regions of northern Europe, Asia, and North America, it thrives in cold climates and boreal forests.

Ecology

Provides early-season nectar for pollinators and food for birds and mammals through its berries.

Cultivation

  • Full sun to partial shade
  • Well-drained, slightly acidic soil
  • Very cold hardy shrub
  • Requires cross-pollination for best fruiting

Cultivars

  • ‘Borealis’ – large sweet berries
  • ‘Tundra’ – high-yield variety
  • ‘Aurora’ – improved flavor selection

Symbolism

Represents resilience, nourishment, and endurance in cold climates.
