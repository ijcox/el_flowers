Lonicera periclymenum – European Honeysuckle

Honeysuckle periclymenum, commonly known as European Honeysuckle, is a fragrant climbing vine native to Europe and parts of North Africa.

Description

This species produces twining woody stems with opposite leaves and clusters of tubular flowers that are creamy white, yellow, and pink, highly fragrant especially at dusk.

Etymology

“Periclymenum” comes from Greek, historically used to describe twining plants.

Taxonomy

Family: Caprifoliaceae
Genus: Lonicera
Species: L. periclymenum

Distribution and Habitat

Native to Europe and western Asia, it grows in hedgerows, woodland edges, and scrublands.

Ecology

Attracts moths, bees, and butterflies, especially nocturnal pollinators drawn to its strong evening scent.

Cultivation

  • Full sun to partial shade
  • Moist, well-drained soil
  • Supports trellises and fences
  • Hardy perennial climber

Cultivars

  • ‘Serotina’ – deeper colored flowers
  • Wild woodland forms

Symbolism

Represents affection, nostalgia, and lasting bonds due to its strong fragrance and entwining growth.
