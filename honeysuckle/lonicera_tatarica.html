Lonicera tatarica – Tatarian Honeysuckle

Honeysuckle tatarica, commonly known as Tatarian Honeysuckle, is a hardy shrub species known for its pink flowers and vigorous growth.

Description

This species forms a dense shrub with opposite leaves and paired tubular flowers ranging from pink to white, followed by bright red berries.

Etymology

“Tatarica” refers to the Tatar region of Eurasia, part of its native distribution area.

Taxonomy

Family: Caprifoliaceae
Genus: Lonicera
Species: L. tatarica

Distribution and Habitat

Native to central Asia and parts of eastern Europe, it has been widely introduced and naturalized in other regions.

Ecology

Its berries are eaten by birds, aiding in seed dispersal, but it can become invasive in non-native habitats.

Cultivation

  • Full sun to partial shade
  • Tolerates poor soils
  • Fast-growing ornamental shrub
  • May require control in gardens

Cultivars

  • Pink flowering forms
  • White-flowered varieties

Symbolism

Represents adaptability and resilience, though sometimes associated with uncontrolled spread.
