Begonia maculata – Polka Dot Begonia

Begonia maculata is a striking foliage plant known for its spotted leaves and decorative appeal in indoor cultivation.

Description

Features elongated olive-green leaves covered with silvery-white spots and deep red undersides. Produces small white to pink flowers.

Etymology

“Maculata” means “spotted,” referring to the distinctive leaf pattern.

Taxonomy

Family: Begoniaceae
Genus: Begonia
Species: B. maculata

Distribution and Habitat

Native to Brazil, growing in tropical forest understories with high humidity.

Ecology

Primarily a foliage plant; flowers attract small pollinators but leaves are the main ecological feature.

Cultivation

  • Bright indirect light
  • High humidity
  • Well-drained, moist soil

Cultivars

  • ‘Wightii’ form

Symbolism

Represents individuality, creativity, and visual contrast in nature.
