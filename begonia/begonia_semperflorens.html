Begonia semperflorens – Wax Begonia Group

Begonia semperflorens is a compact ornamental plant widely used in bedding displays for its continuous flowering.

Description

Small, fleshy-leaved plants producing clusters of white, pink, or red flowers almost year-round in warm climates.

Etymology

“Semperflorens” means “always flowering,” referring to its long blooming period.

Taxonomy

Family: Begoniaceae
Genus: Begonia
Group: Semperflorens hybrids

Distribution and Habitat

Cultivated globally in gardens and containers; derived from South American and Asian ancestors.

Ecology

Primarily ornamental; attracts some pollinators but mainly bred for horticultural use.

Cultivation

  • Partial sun to shade
  • Well-drained soil
  • Regular watering without waterlogging

Cultivars

  • ‘Dragon Wing’ series
  • ‘Cocktail’ series

Symbolism

Represents harmony, gratitude, and gentle beauty.
