Begonia rex – Rex Begonia Group

Begonia rex is a foliage ornamental plant prized for its dramatic leaf patterns rather than its flowers.

Description

Large asymmetrical leaves with striking colors such as silver, purple, pink, and green in spiral and textured patterns.

Etymology

“Rex” means “king,” referring to its highly ornamental and regal foliage appearance.

Taxonomy

Family: Begoniaceae
Genus: Begonia
Group: Rex hybrids

Distribution and Habitat

Native to northeastern India and surrounding regions; now widely cultivated as a houseplant worldwide.

Ecology

Grown primarily for foliage; flowers are small and not the main ornamental feature.

Cultivation

  • Indirect light or shade
  • High humidity
  • Well-drained but moist soil

Cultivars

  • ‘Escargot’
  • ‘Fireworks’
  • ‘Merry Christmas’

Symbolism

Represents individuality, uniqueness, and artistic expression.
