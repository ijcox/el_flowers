Begonia grandis – Hardy Begonia

Begonia grandis is a cold-tolerant perennial begonia known for surviving cooler climates where most begonias cannot.

Description

Medium perennial with pink flowers and attractive green leaves with red undersides. Produces bulbils for self-propagation.

Etymology

“Grandis” means large or great, referring to its relatively robust growth compared to other begonias.

Taxonomy

Family: Begoniaceae
Genus: Begonia
Species: B. grandis

Distribution and Habitat

Native to China and Japan, found in shaded woodland environments and moist rocky slopes.

Ecology

Attracts small pollinators; spreads through underground tubers and bulbils.

Cultivation

  • Partial shade
  • Moist, well-drained soil
  • Mulching improves winter survival

Cultivars

  • ‘Evansiana’ selections

Symbolism

Represents resilience, endurance, and adaptability in changing climates.
