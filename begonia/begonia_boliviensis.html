Begonia boliviensis – Hanging Basket Begonia

Begonia boliviensis is a trailing species widely used in hanging baskets for its cascading flowers and vibrant display.

Description

Produces arching stems with bright orange, red, or pink tubular flowers and narrow, pointed green leaves.

Etymology

Named after Bolivia, where it was first discovered in the wild.

Taxonomy

Family: Begoniaceae
Genus: Begonia
Species: B. boliviensis

Distribution and Habitat

Native to cloud forests in the Andes of Bolivia and Argentina, growing on rocky cliffs and moist slopes.

Ecology

Attracts hummingbirds and insects in its native habitat; adapted to cool, humid environments.

Cultivation

  • Partial shade to filtered sun
  • Well-drained, rich soil
  • Regular watering but not waterlogged

Cultivars

  • ‘Bonfire’
  • ‘Santa Cruz’

Symbolism

Represents joy, abundance, and cascading natural beauty.
