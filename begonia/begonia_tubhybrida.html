Begonia × tuberhybrida – Tuberous Begonia Hybrids

Begonia × tuberhybrida is a group of hybrid begonias grown for their large, showy flowers and tuber-based growth cycle.

Description

Produces large, rose-like flowers in a wide range of colors including red, yellow, white, orange, and pink. Grows from underground tubers that store energy seasonally.

Etymology

“Tuberhybrida” refers to its hybrid origin and tuberous growth form.

Taxonomy

Family: Begoniaceae
Genus: Begonia
Group: Tuberous hybrids (× tuberhybrida)

Distribution and Habitat

Developed from South American Andean species; widely cultivated in temperate ornamental gardening worldwide.

Ecology

Primarily ornamental; attracts pollinators but mainly bred for floral display rather than ecological function.

Cultivation

  • Partial shade
  • Well-drained, fertile soil
  • Dormant dry storage of tubers in winter

Cultivars

  • ‘Nonstop Series’
  • ‘Illumination Series’
  • ‘Picotee forms’

Symbolism

Represents extravagance, beauty, and seasonal transformation.
