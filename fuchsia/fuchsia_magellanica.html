Fuchsia magellanica – Hardy Fuchsia

Fuchsia magellanica is a hardy shrub species known for its elegant hanging flowers and cold tolerance compared to other fuchsias.

Description

A semi-evergreen shrub producing slender, pendulous red and purple flowers that bloom over a long season.

Etymology

Named after the Strait of Magellan region in South America where it was first documented.

Taxonomy

Family: Onagraceae
Genus: Fuchsia
Species: F. magellanica

Distribution and Habitat

Native to southern Chile and Argentina, typically found in forest edges and moist valleys.

Ecology

Pollinated mainly by hummingbirds attracted to its tubular flowers and bright coloration.

Cultivation

  • Partial shade
  • Moist, well-drained soil
  • Hardy in mild temperate climates
  • Long flowering season

Cultivars

  • ‘Riccartonii’ – popular red-purple garden form

Symbolism

Represents grace, confiding love, and elegance.
