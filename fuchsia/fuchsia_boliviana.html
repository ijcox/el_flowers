Fuchsia boliviana – Bolivian Fuchsia

A tropical fuchsia species known for its long red flower clusters and vigorous growth.

Description

A shrub or small tree producing long, hanging clusters of tubular red flowers followed by edible purple fruit.

Etymology

Named after Bolivia, where it is native to Andean forest regions.

Taxonomy

Family: Onagraceae
Genus: Fuchsia
Species: F. boliviana

Distribution and Habitat

Native to Bolivia, Peru, and Ecuador, found in cloud forests and humid montane regions.

Ecology

Pollinated by hummingbirds; fruit is consumed by birds and mammals.

Cultivation

  • Partial shade
  • Humid, well-drained soil
  • Frost-sensitive tropical species
  • Ornamental and fruiting value

Cultivars

  • Mainly wild form in cultivation

Symbolism

Represents abundance, vitality, and tropical beauty.
