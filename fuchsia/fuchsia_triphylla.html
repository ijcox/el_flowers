Fuchsia triphylla – Firecracker Fuchsia

A tropical fuchsia species known for its fiery orange-red tubular flowers arranged in dense clusters.

Description

A compact shrub producing upright clusters of long tubular flowers, typically orange to red, resembling firecrackers.

Etymology

“Triphylla” refers to the plant historically being described with three-leaved characteristics.

Taxonomy

Family: Onagraceae
Genus: Fuchsia
Species: F. triphylla

Distribution and Habitat

Native to the Caribbean, especially Hispaniola, growing in warm tropical forests.

Ecology

Pollinated primarily by hummingbirds attracted to bright tubular flowers and nectar.

Cultivation

  • Warm climates
  • Partial shade
  • Moist, well-drained soil
  • Common parent in hybrid fuchsias

Cultivars

  • Widely used in hybrid breeding programs

Symbolism

Represents energy, vibrancy, and tropical beauty.
