Fuchsia procumbens – Creeping Fuchsia

A low-growing, ground-hugging fuchsia species known for its unusual flowers and decorative berries.

Description

A creeping perennial with tiny upward-facing flowers and striking large red to purple berries that persist after flowering.

Etymology

“Procumbens” means “lying flat,” referring to its creeping growth habit.

Taxonomy

Family: Onagraceae
Genus: Fuchsia
Species: F. procumbens

Distribution and Habitat

Endemic to New Zealand, found in coastal and forest floor habitats.

Ecology

Flowers are pollinated by insects, while berries are dispersed by birds.

Cultivation

  • Partial shade
  • Moist, well-drained soil
  • Cool, mild climates
  • Excellent ground cover plant

Cultivars

  • Mostly wild type in cultivation

Symbolism

Represents subtle beauty, ground-level resilience, and quiet charm.
