Fuchsia regia – Brazilian Fuchsia

A rare fuchsia species known for its striking red flowers and ornamental appeal.

Description

A shrub producing upright to slightly nodding tubular red flowers, often contrasted with dark green foliage.

Etymology

“Regia” means “royal,” referring to its striking and elegant floral display.

Taxonomy

Family: Onagraceae
Genus: Fuchsia
Species: F. regia

Distribution and Habitat

Native to southeastern Brazil, typically found in forested and mountainous regions.

Ecology

Pollinated primarily by hummingbirds attracted to its vivid red tubular flowers.

Cultivation

  • Partial shade
  • Moist, well-drained soil
  • Warm, frost-free climates
  • Ornamental shrub in gardens

Cultivars

  • Limited cultivated forms; mostly species plants

Symbolism

Represents elegance, rarity, and natural grandeur.
