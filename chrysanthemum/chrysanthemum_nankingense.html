Chrysanthemum nankingense – Wild Ancestor Chrysanthemum

Chrysanthemum nankingense is a wild ancestral species that has contributed genetically to many modern cultivated chrysanthemums.

Description

A small perennial herb with simple daisy-like white flowers and yellow centers, closely resembling wild meadow daisies.

Etymology

“Nankingense” refers to Nanjing (historically Nanking), China, where the species was identified and studied.

Taxonomy

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Chrysanthemum
Species: C. nankingense

Distribution and Habitat

Native to eastern China, typically found in grasslands, hillsides, and open disturbed areas.

Ecology

Serves as an important genetic resource for breeding cultivated chrysanthemums and supports native pollinators.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Hardy and adaptable
  • Often used in breeding programs

Cultivars

  • Mainly wild form, not widely cultivated ornamentally

Symbolism

Represents origins, heritage, and natural resilience as a foundational species.
