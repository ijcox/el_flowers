Chrysanthemum arcticum – Arctic Chrysanthemum

Chrysanthemum arcticum is a cold-adapted wild species that grows in northern, subarctic environments.

Description

A low-growing perennial producing white to pale lavender daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and deeply lobed green leaves.

Etymology

“Arcticum” refers to its adaptation to Arctic and subarctic climates.

Taxonomy

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Chrysanthemum
Species: C. arcticum

Distribution and Habitat

Found in Alaska, northern Canada, and parts of northeastern Asia, typically in tundra, rocky coasts, and cold alpine zones.

Ecology

Provides a rare nectar source in extremely cold regions and supports specialized pollinators adapted to short growing seasons.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Cold, well-drained soil
  • Very cold hardy
  • Suitable for alpine gardens

Cultivars

  • Mainly wild species with limited ornamental breeding

Symbolism

Represents survival, purity, and resilience in extreme environments.
