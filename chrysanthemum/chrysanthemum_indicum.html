Chrysanthemum indicum – Indian Chrysanthemum

Chrysanthemum indicum is a hardy wild species that contributed to many modern cultivated chrysanthemum varieties.

Description

A perennial herb producing small to medium yellow flower heads with a simple daisy-like structure and deeply lobed green leaves.

Etymology

“Indicum” refers to India, though the species is native more broadly to East Asia.

Taxonomy

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Chrysanthemum
Species: C. indicum

Distribution and Habitat

Native to China, Japan, and parts of East Asia, growing in grasslands, slopes, and rocky areas.

Ecology

Provides nectar for late-season pollinators and serves as a genetic ancestor for cultivated chrysanthemums.

Cultivation

  • Full sun preferred
  • Well-drained soil
  • Hardy perennial in temperate climates
  • Low maintenance once established

Cultivars

  • Used mainly in breeding rather than direct ornamental cultivation

Symbolism

Represents endurance, natural resilience, and ancestral plant heritage.
