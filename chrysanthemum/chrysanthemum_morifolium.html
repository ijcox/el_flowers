Chrysanthemum morifolium – Florist’s Chrysanthemum

Chrysanthemum morifolium is the main cultivated chrysanthemum used worldwide in floristry for its highly diverse and decorative blooms.

Description

A highly variable perennial producing large, multi-petaled flower heads in a wide range of colors including yellow, white, red, pink, and purple.

Etymology

“Morifolium” refers to mulberry-like leaves in some historical classifications.

Taxonomy

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Chrysanthemum
Species: C. morifolium

Distribution and Habitat

Originally derived from East Asia, now cultivated globally in greenhouses and ornamental gardens.

Ecology

Important for late-season pollinators, though many cultivated forms are bred primarily for appearance rather than ecological function.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Well-drained, fertile soil
  • Pinching required for bushier growth
  • Often grown as seasonal bedding or cut flowers

Cultivars

  • ‘Spider’ chrysanthemums – long, tubular petals
  • ‘Pompon’ types – compact spherical blooms
  • ‘Decorative’ types – large layered flowers

Symbolism

Represents longevity, joy, and celebration in many cultures, especially in East Asia.
