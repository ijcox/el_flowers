Chrysanthemum zawadskii – Siberian Chrysanthemum

Chrysanthemum zawadskii is a cold-hardy wild species native to northern Asia, known for thriving in harsh climates.

Description

A low-growing perennial with small, daisy-like white to pale pink flowers and finely divided foliage.

Etymology

Named in honor of the Polish botanist Aleksander Zawadzki.

Taxonomy

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Chrysanthemum
Species: C. zawadskii

Distribution and Habitat

Native to Siberia, northern China, Korea, and Japan, found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Ecology

Supports pollinators in cold regions where few other flowering plants survive.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Well-drained, poor to average soil
  • Extremely cold hardy
  • Suitable for rock gardens

Cultivars

  • ‘White Bomb’ – dense white blooms
  • ‘Clara Curtis’ – soft pink flowers

Symbolism

Represents endurance, resilience, and survival in extreme environments.
