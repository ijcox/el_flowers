Periwinkle

Periwinkles are evergreen flowering plants valued for their glossy foliage, star-shaped blooms, and reliable ground-cover growth.

Description

Periwinkle flowers usually appear in shades of blue, violet, lavender, or white and have five spreading petals. Trailing stems root as they spread, forming dense mats.

Some plants called periwinkle also refer to Madagascar periwinkle, grown for ornamental and medicinal value.

Taxonomy

Traditional periwinkles belong mainly to the genus Vinca in the family Apocynaceae. Madagascar periwinkle belongs to the genus Catharanthus.

Species

Vinca Minor – Lesser Periwinkle
Vinca Major – Greater Periwinkle
Catharanthus Roseus – Madagascar Periwinkle
Vinca Herbacea – Herbaceous Periwinkle

Etymology

The name Periwinkle developed from older English plant names, while Vinca comes from Latin meaning “to bind,” referring to trailing stems.

Ecology

Periwinkles provide early nectar for pollinators and adapt well to woodland edges, slopes, and shaded garden sites.

Diseases and pests

  • Stem rot in overly wet soil
  • Leaf spot fungal diseases
  • Aphids on tender growth
  • Scale insects on stems

Cultivation

Periwinkle is widely planted as a low-maintenance ground cover and edging plant.

  • Light: Partial shade to full sun
  • Soil: Average, well-drained soil
  • Water: Low to moderate
  • Planting: Divisions or nursery plants
  • Maintenance: Trim to control spread

Periwinkle in culture

Periwinkle symbolizes friendship, enduring affection, memory, and resilience.
