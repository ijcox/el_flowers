Iberis

Iberis, commonly known as candytuft, is a low-growing flowering plant known for its dense clusters of small white or pink blossoms.

Description

Iberis produces compact mounds of flowers that bloom in spring and early summer. Its flowers are often used in rock gardens and borders.

The plant is valued for its evergreen foliage and ground-covering habit.

Taxonomy

Iberis belongs to the genus Iberis in the family Brassicaceae. It is native to southern Europe and the Mediterranean region.

Species

Iberis Sempervirens – Evergreen Candytuft
Iberis Amara – Bitter Candytuft
Iberis Gibraltarica – Gibraltar Candytuft
Iberis Umbellata – Garden Candytuft
Iberis Saxatilis – Rock Candytuft

Etymology

The name Iberis refers to the Iberian Peninsula, where several species are native.

Ecology

Iberis plants attract bees and other pollinators and thrive in dry, rocky environments.

Diseases and pests

  • Root rot in poorly drained soil
  • Aphids on new shoots
  • Downy mildew in humid conditions
  • Fungal leaf spots

Cultivation

Iberis is widely grown as a ground cover and border plant due to its dense flowering habit and low maintenance needs.

  • Light: Full sun
  • Soil: Well-drained, sandy or rocky soil
  • Water: Low to moderate
  • Planting: Seeds or cuttings in spring
  • Maintenance: Trim after flowering to maintain shape

Iberis in culture

Iberis symbolizes purity and resilience due to its bright, simple flowers and ability to thrive in poor soils.
