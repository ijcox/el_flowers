Spider Lily

Spider Lilies are striking flowering plants known for their long, spidery petals and dramatic appearance.

Description

Spider Lily flowers have narrow, curved petals radiating outward with prominent stamens, creating a spider-like shape. They are often red, white, or pink depending on species.

They typically bloom in clusters and are highly ornamental.

Taxonomy

Spider Lilies belong mainly to the genus Lycoris in the family Amaryllidaceae.

Species

Lycoris Radiata – Red Spider Lily
Lycoris Squamigera – Resurrection Lily
Lycoris Albiflora – White Spider Lily

Etymology

The genus name Lycoris is derived from a Roman actress’s name, while the common name refers to the flower’s spider-like shape.

Ecology

Spider Lilies often bloom in late summer or autumn and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Diseases and pests

  • Bulb rot in poorly drained soil
  • Aphids on flower stalks
  • Fungal leaf spot diseases
  • Snails and slugs on young shoots

Cultivation

Spider Lilies are widely grown in ornamental gardens for their dramatic late-season blooms.

  • Light: Full sun to partial shade
  • Soil: Well-drained soil
  • Water: Moderate
  • Planting: Bulbs planted in dormant season
  • Maintenance: Minimal care required

Spider Lily in culture

Spider Lilies symbolize final farewells, remembrance, and rebirth in various cultural traditions.
