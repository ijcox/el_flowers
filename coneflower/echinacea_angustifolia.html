Echinacea angustifolia – Narrow-leaf Purple Coneflower

Coneflower angustifolia is a prairie wildflower species known for its medicinal history and narrow leaves.

Description

A perennial herb with slender leaves and pale purple to pink daisy-like flowers with prominent central cones.

Etymology

“Angustifolia” means “narrow-leaved,” referring to its distinct foliage shape.

Taxonomy

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Echinacea
Species: E. angustifolia

Distribution and Habitat

Native to central North America, especially prairie regions and dry grasslands.

Ecology

Important for native pollinators and historically used by Indigenous peoples for medicinal purposes.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Dry, well-drained soil
  • Drought tolerant
  • Long-lived perennial in prairie gardens

Cultivars

  • Mainly wild-type form in cultivation

Symbolism

Represents healing, endurance, and natural strength.
