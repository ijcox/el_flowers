Echinacea paradoxa – Yellow Coneflower

Coneflower paradoxa is the only naturally occurring yellow-flowered species in the genus Echinacea.

Description

A distinctive perennial producing bright yellow petals surrounding a dark central cone, contrasting with most purple-flowered coneflowers.

Etymology

“Paradoxa” refers to its unusual yellow color, which is unexpected within the genus.

Taxonomy

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Echinacea
Species: E. paradoxa

Distribution and Habitat

Native to the south-central United States, typically found in prairies and open woodland areas.

Ecology

Provides nectar and pollen for bees and butterflies and contributes genetic diversity to cultivated hybrids.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Drought tolerant
  • Useful in breeding programs

Cultivars

  • Often used in hybrid coneflower breeding

Symbolism

Represents uniqueness, rarity, and natural variation.
