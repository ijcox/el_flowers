Echinacea pallida – Pale Purple Coneflower

Coneflower pallida is a prairie species known for its slender drooping petals and soft pale coloration.

Description

A perennial wildflower with narrow, pale pink to light purple petals that droop sharply downward from a prominent central cone.

Etymology

“Pallida” means “pale,” referring to its light-colored petals compared to other Echinacea species.

Taxonomy

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Echinacea
Species: E. pallida

Distribution and Habitat

Native to central North America, especially tallgrass prairies and open fields.

Ecology

Supports bees, butterflies, and other pollinators in prairie ecosystems during summer bloom periods.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Dry to medium well-drained soil
  • Drought tolerant once established
  • Best in naturalized prairie plantings

Cultivars

  • Mainly grown as wild species form

Symbolism

Represents simplicity, resilience, and quiet strength.
