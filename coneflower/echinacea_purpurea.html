Echinacea purpurea – Purple Coneflower

Coneflower purpurea is a widely cultivated North American wildflower valued for its medicinal uses and striking purple blooms.

Description

A perennial herb producing large daisy-like purple flowers with raised orange-brown central cones and drooping petals.

Etymology

“Echinacea” comes from Greek “echinos,” meaning hedgehog, referring to the spiny central cone.

Taxonomy

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Echinacea
Species: E. purpurea

Distribution and Habitat

Native to eastern and central North America, commonly found in prairies and open woodlands.

Ecology

Highly attractive to bees, butterflies, and other pollinators, especially in mid to late summer.

Cultivation

  • Full sun preferred
  • Well-drained soil
  • Drought tolerant once established
  • Low maintenance perennial

Cultivars

  • ‘Magnus’ – large vibrant purple blooms
  • ‘PowWow White’ – white-flowered selection
  • ‘Green Twister’ – green and pink bicolored petals

Symbolism

Represents strength, healing, and resilience.
