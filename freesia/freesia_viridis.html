Freesia viridis – Green Freesia

A rare freesia species distinguished by its unusual green-tinged flowers.

Description

A delicate bulbous perennial producing small, often green or green-white flowers on thin arching stems.

Etymology

“Viridis” means “green,” referring to its unusual flower coloration.

Taxonomy

Family: Iridaceae
Genus: Freesia
Species: F. viridis

Distribution and Habitat

Native to South Africa, typically found in sandy soils and seasonal rainfall regions.

Ecology

Likely pollinated by small insects adapted to subtle floral cues.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Warm, dry summers preferred
  • Grown mainly by collectors

Cultivars

  • Few or no commercial cultivars; mostly wild type

Symbolism

Represents rarity, subtle beauty, and uniqueness.
