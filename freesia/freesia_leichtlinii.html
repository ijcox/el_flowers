Freesia leichtlinii – Yellow Freesia

A bright yellow-flowering freesia species valued for its ornamental and breeding importance.

Description

A bulbous perennial producing graceful arching stems with funnel-shaped yellow flowers, often strongly scented.

Etymology

Named after botanist Max Leichtlin, who contributed to horticultural plant introductions.

Taxonomy

Family: Iridaceae
Genus: Freesia
Species: F. leichtlinii

Distribution and Habitat

Native to South Africa, growing in sandy soils and Mediterranean-type climates.

Ecology

Pollinated by insects attracted to its bright coloration and fragrance.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Grown from corms
  • Common in hybrid freesia breeding

Cultivars

  • ‘Golden Yellow’ – strong garden selection

Symbolism

Represents joy, friendship, and optimism.
