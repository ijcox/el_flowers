Freesia refracta – Common Freesia

Freesia refracta is a fragrant flowering plant in the iris family, widely cultivated for its colorful and scented blooms.

Description

A bulbous perennial producing arching stems with funnel-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, white, and orange, known for strong fragrance.

Etymology

Named after German botanist Friedrich Freese; “refracta” refers to the bent or curved flower stems.

Taxonomy

Family: Iridaceae
Genus: Freesia
Species: F. refracta

Distribution and Habitat

Native to South Africa, growing in sandy or rocky soils in Mediterranean-type climates.

Ecology

Pollinated by insects attracted to its fragrance and bright colors.

Cultivation

  • Full sun to light shade
  • Well-drained soil
  • Grown from corms
  • Popular cut flower species

Cultivars

  • ‘Double Yellow’ – fuller blooms
  • ‘Golden Wave’ – bright yellow selection

Symbolism

Represents friendship, trust, and innocence.
