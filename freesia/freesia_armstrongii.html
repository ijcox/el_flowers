Freesia armstrongii – Wild Freesia

A wild South African freesia species known for its delicate flowers and role in hybrid freesia development.

Description

A small bulbous perennial producing slender stems with lightly scented flowers, usually pale purple, pink, or white.

Etymology

Named in honor of botanist Armstrong, associated with South African flora studies.

Taxonomy

Family: Iridaceae
Genus: Freesia
Species: F. armstrongii

Distribution and Habitat

Native to South Africa, found in open grasslands and rocky slopes.

Ecology

Pollinated by insects; contributes genetic diversity to cultivated freesia hybrids.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Well-drained sandy soil
  • Grown from corms
  • Used in hybrid breeding

Cultivars

  • Mainly used in hybridization rather than direct ornamental cultivation

Symbolism

Represents wild beauty and botanical heritage.
