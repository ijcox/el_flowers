Freesia alba – White Freesia

A white-flowering form of Freesia known for its purity of color and strong fragrance.

Description

A bulbous perennial producing elegant white funnel-shaped flowers on arching stems, highly fragrant and often used in perfumery and bouquets.

Etymology

“Alba” means “white,” referring to its pure white blooms.

Taxonomy

Family: Iridaceae
Genus: Freesia
Species: F. alba

Distribution and Habitat

Native to South Africa, typically found in sandy soils and coastal regions with mild climates.

Ecology

Attracts bees and other pollinators, especially in cultivated gardens.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Grown from corms
  • Popular ornamental and cut flower

Cultivars

  • ‘White Pearl’ – large pure white blooms

Symbolism

Represents purity, innocence, and elegance.
