Prunus yedoensis – Yoshino Cherry

Cherry Blossom yedoensis, commonly known as Yoshino cherry, is one of the most widely planted ornamental cherry trees in the world.

Description

A deciduous tree producing masses of pale pink to nearly white blossoms in early spring, often before leaf emergence, creating a soft cloud-like appearance.

Etymology

“Yedoensis” refers to Edo, the former name of Tokyo, where the tree became widely cultivated.

Taxonomy

Family: Rosaceae
Genus: Prunus
Species: P. yedoensis

Distribution and Habitat

Believed to have originated in Japan, now widely planted in temperate regions across parks, riversides, and urban landscapes.

Ecology

Provides early spring nectar for pollinators. Its mass blooming supports seasonal insect activity.

Cultivation

  • Full sun preferred
  • Well-drained, fertile soil
  • Moderate watering
  • Often planted in large ornamental groups

Cultivars

  • ‘Somei Yoshino’ – most common cultivated form
  • Clonal selections for uniform flowering

Symbolism

Represents renewal, transience, and the beauty of springtime in Japanese culture.
