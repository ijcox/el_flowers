Prunus amanogawa – Columnar Cherry

Cherry Blossom amanogawa is a narrow, upright ornamental cherry tree prized for its elegant column-like growth habit.

Description

A slender, upright-growing cherry tree producing pale pink, lightly fragrant blossoms that appear along its vertical branches in spring.

Etymology

“Amanogawa” means “Milky Way” in Japanese, referring to its tall, narrow form resembling a vertical starry stream.

Taxonomy

Family: Rosaceae
Genus: Prunus
Species: P. amanogawa

Distribution and Habitat

Developed in Japan as an ornamental cultivar, widely planted in small gardens and urban landscapes where space is limited.

Ecology

Provides spring nectar for pollinators and is mainly cultivated for ornamental rather than ecological value.

Cultivation

  • Prefers full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Compact growth suitable for narrow spaces
  • Low maintenance once established

Cultivars

  • Mainly cultivated as a single ornamental form

Symbolism

Represents elegance, aspiration, and upward movement, often associated with refined garden design.
