Prunus serrulata – Japanese Cherry

Cherry Blossom serrulata is one of the most iconic ornamental cherry trees, celebrated for its spectacular springtime flowering display.

Description

A deciduous tree producing abundant clusters of pale pink to deep pink blossoms. Flowers often appear before or alongside young leaves, creating a dense floral canopy.

Etymology

“Serrulata” refers to the finely serrated edges of its leaves.

Taxonomy

Family: Rosaceae
Genus: Prunus
Species: P. serrulata

Distribution and Habitat

Native to Japan, Korea, and China, widely cultivated in temperate regions for ornamental planting in parks and streets.

Ecology

Provides early spring nectar and pollen for bees. The blossoms are short-lived but support seasonal pollinator activity.

Cultivation

  • Prefers full sun
  • Well-drained, fertile soil
  • Moderate watering
  • Pruned after flowering to maintain shape

Cultivars

  • ‘Kanzan’ – double pink flowers (widely planted)
  • ‘Amanogawa’ – columnar form
  • ‘Shirofugen’ – late white-to-pink blooms

Symbolism

Represents beauty, renewal, and the fleeting nature of life, especially in Japanese culture.
