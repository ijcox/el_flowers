Prunus subhirtella – Higan Cherry

Cherry Blossom subhirtella, commonly known as Higan cherry, is a graceful ornamental cherry valued for its early and sometimes autumnal flowering.

Description

A medium-sized deciduous tree with arching branches producing delicate pale pink to white blossoms. It is known for flowering both in spring and occasionally again in autumn.

Etymology

“Subhirtella” refers to the slightly hairy (pubescent) features of young leaves and shoots.

Taxonomy

Family: Rosaceae
Genus: Prunus
Species: P. subhirtella

Distribution and Habitat

Native to Japan, growing in forest edges and hillsides, and widely cultivated in temperate ornamental landscapes.

Ecology

Provides early and late-season nectar sources for pollinators, extending flowering availability beyond many other cherries.

Cultivation

  • Prefers full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Hardy and adaptable to various climates
  • Used in ornamental avenue planting

Cultivars

  • ‘Autumnalis’ – blooms in spring and autumn
  • ‘Pendula’ – weeping form

Symbolism

Represents continuity, seasonal change, and delicate beauty across multiple times of the year.
