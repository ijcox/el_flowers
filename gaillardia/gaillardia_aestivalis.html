Gaillardia aestivalis – Prairie Gaillardia

Gaillardia aestivalis is a native North American wildflower known for its subtle beauty and adaptability to prairie environments.

Description

A perennial plant producing smaller, more delicate flower heads compared to other Gaillardia species, often with soft yellow or pale hues.

Etymology

“Aestivalis” means “of summer,” referring to its seasonal flowering period.

Taxonomy

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Gaillardia
Species: G. aestivalis

Distribution and Habitat

Native to the southeastern United States, typically found in prairies, open woodlands, and sandy soils.

Ecology

Supports native pollinators such as bees and butterflies and thrives in warm, dry climates.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Well-drained sandy soil
  • Drought tolerant
  • Ideal for naturalized prairie plantings

Cultivars

  • Rarely cultivated; mostly grown in native plant gardens

Symbolism

Represents simplicity, resilience, and natural harmony.
