Gaillardia aristata – Perennial Blanket Flower

Gaillardia aristata is a hardy perennial known for its long-lasting, brightly colored daisy-like flowers.

Description

A clump-forming perennial producing large flower heads with red centers and yellow or orange petals, blooming through summer.

Etymology

“Aristata” refers to bristle-like structures on the plant’s seeds.

Taxonomy

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Gaillardia
Species: G. aristata

Distribution and Habitat

Native to North America, particularly the Great Plains and western regions, growing in prairies and open grasslands.

Ecology

Provides nectar for bees and butterflies; tolerant of dry conditions and poor soils.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Drought tolerant
  • Long blooming season

Cultivars

  • ‘Goblin’ – compact form
  • ‘Arizona Sun’ – early blooming hybrid

Symbolism

Represents strength, endurance, and warmth.
