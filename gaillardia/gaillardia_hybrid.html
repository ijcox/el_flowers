Gaillardia × hybrida – Garden Hybrid Blanket Flower

Gaillardia × hybrida represents cultivated hybrids developed for ornamental gardens, combining traits from multiple Gaillardia species.

Description

A colorful perennial producing large, long-lasting daisy-like blooms in combinations of red, orange, and yellow.

Etymology

“Hybrid” indicates its origin from cross-breeding between different Gaillardia species.

Taxonomy

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Gaillardia
Hybrid: G. × hybrida

Distribution and Habitat

Cultivated worldwide in gardens; not naturally occurring in the wild.

Ecology

Attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies; tolerant of heat and dry conditions.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Drought tolerant
  • Long blooming season with deadheading

Cultivars

  • ‘Arizona Sun’ – compact and early blooming
  • ‘Burgundy’ – deep red flowers
  • ‘Fanfare’ – trumpet-shaped petals

Symbolism

Represents creativity, vitality, and cultivated beauty.
