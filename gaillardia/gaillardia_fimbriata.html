Gaillardia fimbriata – Fringed Blanket Flower

Gaillardia fimbriata is a lesser-known species recognized for its distinctive fringed petal edges.

Description

A flowering plant with daisy-like blooms, often featuring fringed or finely divided petal tips, giving a textured appearance.

Etymology

“Fimbriata” means “fringed,” referring to the edges of its petals.

Taxonomy

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Gaillardia
Species: G. fimbriata

Distribution and Habitat

Found in parts of North America, typically in open plains and dry habitats.

Ecology

Attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies and tolerates dry conditions.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Drought tolerant
  • Suitable for wildflower gardens

Cultivars

  • Rarely cultivated; mostly found in wild or specialty collections

Symbolism

Represents individuality, texture, and natural diversity.
