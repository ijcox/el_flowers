Gaillardia pulchella – Firewheel

Gaillardia pulchella is a vibrant annual wildflower known for its red and yellow daisy-like blooms.

Description

A fast-growing annual producing brightly colored flower heads with red centers and yellow-tipped petals.

Etymology

“Pulchella” means “beautiful,” referring to its striking flower coloration.

Taxonomy

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Gaillardia
Species: G. pulchella

Distribution and Habitat

Native to the southern United States and Mexico, often found in sandy soils and open plains.

Ecology

Attracts bees, butterflies, and other pollinators; thrives in dry, sunny environments.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Drought tolerant
  • Easy to grow from seed

Cultivars

  • ‘Lorenziana’ – double-flowered form
  • ‘Red Plume’ – deep red blooms

Symbolism

Represents warmth, vibrancy, and enduring cheerfulness.
