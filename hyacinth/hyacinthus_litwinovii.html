Hyacinthus litwinovii – Litwinov’s Hyacinth

Hyacinth litwinovii, commonly known as Litwinov’s Hyacinth, is a rare wild species valued for its delicate appearance and early spring flowering.

Description

This species produces slender stems with loosely arranged pale blue to lilac flowers, less dense than cultivated hyacinths.

Etymology

Named in honor of Russian botanist Dmitry Litvinov, who contributed to the study of Eurasian flora.

Taxonomy

Family: Asparagaceae
Genus: Hyacinthus
Species: H. litwinovii

Distribution and Habitat

Native to Central Asia, particularly mountainous and rocky regions with well-drained soils.

Ecology

Provides early nectar for pollinators in alpine and subalpine ecosystems.

Cultivation

  • Full sun preferred
  • Well-drained rocky soil
  • Cold tolerant species
  • Best suited for rock gardens

Cultivars

  • Wild forms only widely known
  • Limited horticultural selection

Symbolism

Represents rarity, quiet beauty, and resilience in harsh environments.
