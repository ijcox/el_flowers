Kalanchoe

Kalanchoe is a genus of succulent flowering plants known for their thick leaves and clusters of small, vibrant flowers.

Description

Kalanchoe plants are drought-tolerant succulents that produce long-lasting flower clusters in red, pink, orange, yellow, and white.

They are popular as indoor houseplants due to their low maintenance needs.

Taxonomy

Kalanchoe belongs to the genus Kalanchoe in the family Crassulaceae. It is native to Madagascar and tropical Africa.

Species

Kalanchoe Blossfeldiana – Flaming Katy
Kalanchoe Tomentosa – Panda Plant
Kalanchoe Pinnata – Cathedral Bells
Kalanchoe Daigremontiana – Mother of Thousands

Etymology

The name Kalanchoe is believed to come from Chinese origin, referring to ornamental flowering plants.

Ecology

Kalanchoe species are adapted to dry climates and store water in their leaves for survival in arid conditions.

Diseases and pests

  • Mealybugs on leaves
  • Root rot from overwatering
  • Aphids on flower buds
  • Powdery mildew in humid conditions

Cultivation

Kalanchoe is widely cultivated as an ornamental indoor and outdoor succulent.

  • Light: Bright indirect light
  • Soil: Well-draining succulent soil
  • Water: Low; allow soil to dry between watering
  • Planting: Cuttings or offsets
  • Maintenance: Remove spent flowers

Kalanchoe in culture

Kalanchoe symbolizes endurance and persistence due to its ability to thrive in harsh environments.
