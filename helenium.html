Helenium

Helenium, commonly known as sneezeweed, is a group of bright late-summer flowering plants known for their daisy-like blooms in warm tones.

Description

Helenium flowers feature rounded petals in shades of yellow, orange, and red with raised central discs. They bloom late in the growing season, providing color when many other plants fade.

Despite its name, it does not cause allergies; the name comes from historical medicinal use.

Taxonomy

Helenium belongs to the genus Helenium in the family Asteraceae. It is native to North and Central America.

Species

Helenium Autumnale – Common Sneezeweed
Helenium Bigelovii – Bigelow’s Sneezeweed
Helenium Hoopesii – Orange Sneezeweed
Helenium Flexuosum – Purplehead Sneezeweed
Helenium Virginicum – Virginia Sneezeweed

Etymology

The name Helenium is traditionally linked to Helen of Troy, with legend suggesting the flowers grew where her tears fell.

Ecology

Helenium plants are valuable nectar sources for bees, butterflies, and other late-season pollinators.

Diseases and pests

  • Aphids on flower heads
  • Powdery mildew in humid climates
  • Leaf spot fungal infections
  • Root rot in poorly drained soil

Cultivation

Helenium is widely cultivated for ornamental borders and pollinator gardens due to its vibrant late-season blooms.

  • Light: Full sun
  • Soil: Moist, well-drained soil
  • Water: Moderate to high
  • Planting: Seeds or division in spring
  • Maintenance: Deadhead to extend flowering

Helenium in culture

Helenium symbolizes resilience and warmth, often associated with the late summer and autumn garden palette.
