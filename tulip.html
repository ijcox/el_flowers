Tulip

Tulips are classic spring flowers known for their simple cup-shaped blooms and vibrant colors.

Description

Tulip flowers grow singly on upright stems and come in nearly every color except true blue. Petals form a smooth cup or sometimes fringed or double shapes.

They are widely cultivated for ornamental gardens and bulb farming.

Taxonomy

Tulips belong to the genus Tulipa in the family Liliaceae. They are native to Central Asia and the Mediterranean region.

Species

Tulipa Gesneriana – Garden Tulip
Tulipa Sylvestris – Wild Tulip
Tulipa Clusiana – Lady Tulip

Etymology

The name Tulipa comes from the Persian word for turban, referring to the flower’s shape.

Ecology

Tulips are early spring bloomers and provide nectar for bees and other insects.

Diseases and pests

  • Bulb rot in wet soil
  • Aphids on stems
  • Slugs damaging leaves
  • Fungal infections in storage

Cultivation

Tulips are widely grown from bulbs and are a major feature of spring flower displays.

  • Light: Full sun
  • Soil: Well-drained soil
  • Water: Moderate
  • Planting: Bulbs in autumn
  • Maintenance: Allow foliage to die back naturally

Tulip in culture

Tulips symbolize perfect love, elegance, and renewal, especially associated with spring festivals.
