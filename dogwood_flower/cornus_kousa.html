Cornus kousa – Kousa Dogwood

Dogwood kousa is an ornamental tree valued for its layered structure and late-spring star-like floral display.

Description

A small deciduous tree producing pointed white bracts around tiny flowers, followed by edible red berry-like fruits.

Etymology

“Kousa” is derived from the Japanese name for this species.

Taxonomy

Family: Cornaceae
Genus: Cornus
Species: C. kousa

Distribution and Habitat

Native to East Asia (Japan, Korea, China), growing in woodland edges and forested hills.

Ecology

Fruits are consumed by birds and mammals, aiding seed dispersal.

Cultivation

  • Full sun to partial shade
  • Well-drained, acidic soil
  • More disease-resistant than C. florida
  • Slow to moderate growth rate

Cultivars

  • ‘China Girl’ – heavy flowering selection
  • ‘Milky Way’ – prolific bloom and fruiting

Symbolism

Represents endurance, elegance, and harmony.
