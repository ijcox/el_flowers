Cornus sericea – Red Osier Dogwood

Dogwood sericea is a hardy shrub known for its striking red stems, especially visible in winter landscapes.

Description

A multi-stemmed deciduous shrub producing clusters of white flowers in spring, white berries in summer, and bright red stems in winter.

Etymology

“Sericea” refers to the silky texture of young stems and leaves.

Taxonomy

Family: Cornaceae
Genus: Cornus
Species: C. sericea

Distribution and Habitat

Native to North America, commonly found along wetlands, rivers, and forest edges.

Ecology

Important for wildlife: berries feed birds and dense stems provide shelter.

Cultivation

  • Full sun to partial shade
  • Moist to wet soil conditions
  • Excellent for erosion control
  • Very cold hardy

Cultivars

  • ‘Cardinal’ – vivid red stem selection
  • ‘Flaviramea’ – yellow-stemmed variety

Symbolism

Represents resilience, protection, and seasonal beauty.
