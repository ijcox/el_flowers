Cornus florida – Flowering Dogwood

Dogwood florida is a small ornamental tree admired for its showy spring “flowers,” which are actually modified bracts surrounding small true flowers.

Description

A deciduous tree producing white to pink bracts in spring, followed by red berry-like drupes and attractive autumn foliage.

Etymology

“Florida” means “flowering” or “abundant in flowers,” referring to its striking spring display.

Taxonomy

Family: Cornaceae
Genus: Cornus
Species: C. florida

Distribution and Habitat

Native to eastern North America, commonly found in woodland understories and forest edges.

Ecology

Provides nectar for pollinators and fruit for birds and small mammals.

Cultivation

  • Partial shade to full sun
  • Well-drained, slightly acidic soil
  • Prefers sheltered locations
  • Susceptible to dogwood anthracnose in some regions

Cultivars

  • ‘Rubra’ – pink-bracted form
  • ‘Cherokee Chief’ – deep red selection

Symbolism

Represents rebirth, purity, and resilience.
