Cornus mas – Cornelian Cherry Dogwood

Dogwood mas is an early-flowering dogwood species valued for its yellow blossoms and edible red fruit.

Description

A deciduous shrub or small tree producing clusters of small yellow flowers in late winter, followed by cherry-like red fruits.

Etymology

“Mas” refers historically to male or robust form, though its exact origin is debated.

Taxonomy

Family: Cornaceae
Genus: Cornus
Species: C. mas

Distribution and Habitat

Native to southern Europe and western Asia, often found in woodland edges and scrublands.

Ecology

Early flowering provides nectar for pollinators when few other plants are in bloom.

Cultivation

  • Full sun to partial shade
  • Well-drained soil
  • Drought tolerant once established
  • Cold hardy and long-lived

Cultivars

  • ‘Aurea’ – yellow foliage form

Symbolism

Represents endurance, early renewal, and nourishment.
