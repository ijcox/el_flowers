Cornus nuttallii – Pacific Dogwood

Dogwood nuttallii is a striking flowering tree native to western North America, known for its large showy bracts.

Description

A medium-sized deciduous tree producing large white to pinkish bracts surrounding small clustered flowers, followed by red fruit.

Etymology

Named after botanist Thomas Nuttall, who studied North American flora.

Taxonomy

Family: Cornaceae
Genus: Cornus
Species: C. nuttallii

Distribution and Habitat

Native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, found in coastal forests and mountain slopes.

Ecology

Provides food for birds and mammals, and supports forest pollinators during spring bloom.

Cultivation

  • Partial shade preferred
  • Moist, well-drained soil
  • Cool, mild climates
  • Can be sensitive to heat stress

Cultivars

  • ‘Goldspot’ – variegated foliage form

Symbolism

Represents natural beauty, resilience, and regional identity.
