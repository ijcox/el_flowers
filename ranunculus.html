Ranunculus

Ranunculus are elegant flowering plants admired for their densely layered petals and rose-like blooms.

Description

Flowers are cup-shaped or fully double with many thin petals arranged in spirals. They appear in white, yellow, orange, pink, red, and purple shades.

The foliage is finely divided and many species grow from tubers or fibrous roots.

Taxonomy

Ranunculus belongs to the genus Ranunculus in the family Ranunculaceae. The genus includes hundreds of species worldwide.

Species

Ranunculus Asiaticus – Persian Buttercup
Ranunculus Acris – Meadow Buttercup
Ranunculus Repens – Creeping Buttercup
Ranunculus Bulbosus – Bulbous Buttercup

Etymology

The name Ranunculus comes from Latin meaning “little frog,” referring to the moist habitats of many wild species.

Ecology

Wild ranunculus species provide nectar and pollen for insects and often grow in meadows, marshes, and open grasslands.

Diseases and pests

  • Aphids on buds and stems
  • Powdery mildew in humid weather
  • Root rot in wet soils
  • Slugs feeding on young leaves

Cultivation

Florist ranunculus are prized cut flowers and popular spring bedding plants.

  • Light: Full sun
  • Soil: Rich, well-drained soil
  • Water: Moderate
  • Planting: Tubers planted in cool seasons
  • Maintenance: Protect from extreme heat

Ranunculus in culture

Ranunculus symbolizes charm, radiant attraction, and elegance.
