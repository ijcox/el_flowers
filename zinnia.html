Zinnia

Zinnias are bright, cheerful flowering plants known for their bold colors and long-lasting blooms.

Description

Zinnia flowers come in a wide range of colors including red, pink, orange, yellow, white, and purple. They are daisy-like in shape and grow on upright stems.

They are popular in gardens due to their continuous blooming and ease of growth.

Taxonomy

Zinnias belong to the genus Zinnia in the family Asteraceae. They are native to the Americas.

Species

Zinnia Elegans – Common Zinnia
Zinnia Angustifolia – Narrowleaf Zinnia
Zinnia Haageana – Mexican Zinnia

Etymology

The genus name Zinnia honors the German botanist Johann Zinn.

Ecology

Zinnias attract bees, butterflies, and hummingbirds, making them excellent pollinator plants.

Diseases and pests

  • Powdery mildew in humid climates
  • Aphids on stems and buds
  • Spider mites in dry conditions
  • Leaf spot fungal infections

Cultivation

Zinnias are easy to grow annuals widely used in flower beds and cut flower gardens.

  • Light: Full sun
  • Soil: Well-drained soil
  • Water: Moderate
  • Planting: Direct sow seeds after frost
  • Maintenance: Deadhead for continuous blooms

Zinnia in culture

Zinnias symbolize friendship, endurance, and lasting affection.
