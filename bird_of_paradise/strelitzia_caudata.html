Strelitzia caudata – Mountain Bird of Paradise

Bird of Paradise caudata is a lesser-known species adapted to higher elevations and rugged mountain environments.

Description

Robust plant with large banana-like leaves and pale to white bird-like flowers emerging from dark bracts.

Etymology

“Caudata” means “tailed,” referring to elongated structures in parts of the plant’s flower anatomy.

Taxonomy

Family: Strelitziaceae
Genus: Strelitzia
Species: S. caudata

Distribution and Habitat

Native to southern Africa, especially mountainous and cliffside regions.

Ecology

Likely bird-pollinated; adapted to cooler, high-altitude environments compared to other species.

Cultivation

  • Warm but not extreme heat
  • Well-drained soil
  • Protection from strong winds

Cultivars

  • Rare in cultivation; mostly botanical collections

Symbolism

Represents endurance, elevation, and natural grandeur.
