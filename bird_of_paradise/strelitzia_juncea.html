Strelitzia juncea – Rush-leaved Bird of Paradise

Bird of Paradise juncea is a drought-tolerant species known for its reed-like leaves and sculptural form.

Description

Distinctive species with narrow, cylindrical leaf stems resembling rushes and bright orange-blue bird-like flowers.

Etymology

“Juncea” means “rush-like,” referring to its leaf shape.

Taxonomy

Family: Strelitziaceae
Genus: Strelitzia
Species: S. juncea

Distribution and Habitat

Native to South Africa, found in dry, rocky coastal and inland regions.

Ecology

Pollinated by birds; adapted to arid conditions with reduced leaf surface area.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Well-drained sandy soil
  • Drought tolerant once established

Cultivars

  • Mainly wild-type in cultivation

Symbolism

Represents resilience, minimalism, and adaptation.
