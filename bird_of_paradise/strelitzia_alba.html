Strelitzia alba – White Flowering Species

Bird of Paradise alba is a rare, tree-like tropical species producing pale to white flowers.

Description

Large evergreen plant with broad leaves and pale white to cream flowers emerging from boat-shaped bracts.

Etymology

“Alba” means “white,” referring to its pale floral coloration.

Taxonomy

Family: Strelitziaceae
Genus: Strelitzia
Species: S. alba

Distribution and Habitat

Native to southern Africa, typically found in coastal and forest margins.

Ecology

Likely bird-pollinated in native environments, though less studied than other species.

Cultivation

  • Warm climates only
  • Full sun to partial shade
  • Well-drained soil

Cultivars

  • Rare in cultivation; mostly botanical specimens

Symbolism

Represents purity, rarity, and understated elegance.
