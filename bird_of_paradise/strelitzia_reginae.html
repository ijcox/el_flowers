Strelitzia reginae – Orange Bird of Paradise

Bird of Paradise reginae is a striking tropical plant known for its crane-like flowers resembling a bird in flight.

Description

Evergreen perennial with stiff leaves and bright orange and blue flowers shaped like a bird’s head and crest.

Etymology

“Reginae” means “of the queen,” named in honor of Queen Charlotte of Mecklenburg-Strelitz.

Taxonomy

Family: Strelitziaceae
Genus: Strelitzia
Species: S. reginae

Distribution and Habitat

Native to South Africa, growing in coastal scrub and riverbank habitats.

Ecology

Pollinated mainly by sunbirds; adapted to warm climates with seasonal rainfall.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Moderate watering

Cultivars

  • ‘Mandela’s Gold’

Symbolism

Represents joy, freedom, and paradise-like beauty.
