Jonquil

Jonquils are fragrant spring-flowering plants closely related to daffodils, known for their clusters of small, bright yellow blooms.

Description

Jonquils produce multiple flowers per stem, each with a sweet fragrance. They are smaller and more clustered than many other narcissus types.

They are popular in gardens and naturalized spring landscapes.

Taxonomy

Jonquils belong to the genus Narcissus in the family Amaryllidaceae. They are native to the Mediterranean region.

Species

Narcissus Jonquilla – True Jonquil
Narcissus Recurvus – Pheasant’s Eye
Narcissus Odorus – Sweet-scented Jonquil
Narcissus Tazetta – Paperwhite-type Jonquil

Etymology

The name Jonquil comes from the Spanish “junquillo,” meaning little rush, referring to its narrow leaves.

Ecology

Jonquils provide early-season nectar for bees and thrive in open grasslands and Mediterranean climates.

Diseases and pests

  • Bulb rot in wet soil
  • Narcissus fly larvae damage
  • Aphids on flower stems
  • Fungal leaf spot diseases

Cultivation

Jonquils are widely cultivated for naturalized planting and spring garden displays.

  • Light: Full sun to partial shade
  • Soil: Well-drained soil
  • Water: Moderate during growth
  • Planting: Bulbs planted in autumn
  • Maintenance: Allow foliage to die back naturally

Jonquil in culture

Jonquils symbolize desire, renewal, and springtime joy and are often associated with early seasonal gardens.
