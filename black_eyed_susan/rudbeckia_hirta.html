Rudbeckia hirta – Common Black-Eyed Susan

Black-Eyed Susan hirta is a bright, cheerful wildflower known for its golden petals and dark central cone.

Description

Produces daisy-like yellow to orange flowers with a dark brown central disk on hairy stems.

Etymology

“Rudbeckia” honors Swedish botanist Olof Rudbeck; “hirta” means hairy.

Taxonomy

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Rudbeckia
Species: R. hirta

Distribution and Habitat

Native to North America, found in prairies, meadows, and roadsides.

Ecology

Important nectar source for bees, butterflies, and other pollinators.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Drought tolerant once established

Cultivars

  • ‘Goldsturm’
  • ‘Cherokee Sunset’

Symbolism

Represents encouragement, justice, and optimism.
