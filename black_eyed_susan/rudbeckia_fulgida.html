Rudbeckia fulgida – Orange Coneflower Type

Black-Eyed Susan fulgida is a perennial wildflower known for its long blooming season and bright golden flowers.

Description

Produces abundant orange-yellow daisy-like flowers with dark central cones on upright stems.

Etymology

“Fulgida” means “shining” or “glowing,” referring to its bright flower display.

Taxonomy

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Rudbeckia
Species: R. fulgida

Distribution and Habitat

Native to eastern and central North America, commonly found in meadows and open woodlands.

Ecology

Attracts bees, butterflies, and seed-eating birds in late season.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Adaptable soil conditions
  • Very hardy and low maintenance

Cultivars

  • ‘Goldsturm’ (very common garden variety)

Symbolism

Represents endurance, positivity, and radiant energy.
