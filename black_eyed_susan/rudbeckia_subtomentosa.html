Rudbeckia subtomentosa – Sweet Black-Eyed Susan

Black-Eyed Susan subtomentosa is a tall, fragrant species known for its soft-textured foliage and sweet scent.

Description

Produces pale yellow to golden daisy-like flowers with dark centers and slightly aromatic leaves.

Etymology

“Subtomentosa” means “somewhat fuzzy,” referring to the fine hairs on its leaves.

Taxonomy

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Rudbeckia
Species: R. subtomentosa

Distribution and Habitat

Native to central United States, found in prairies and moist meadows.

Ecology

Supports bees, butterflies, and other pollinators with late-summer nectar.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Moist but well-drained soil
  • Tolerates drought once established

Cultivars

  • ‘Henry Eilers’

Symbolism

Represents warmth, harmony, and gentle strength.
