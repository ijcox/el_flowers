Rudbeckia triloba – Brown-Eyed Susan

Black-Eyed Susan triloba is a short-lived perennial or biennial known for its profuse branching and smaller, abundant flowers.

Description

Produces many small yellow daisy-like flowers with dark brown centers on highly branched stems.

Etymology

“Triloba” refers to its three-lobed leaves.

Taxonomy

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Rudbeckia
Species: R. triloba

Distribution and Habitat

Native to North America, found in fields, prairies, and disturbed soils.

Ecology

Excellent pollinator plant supporting bees, butterflies, and beneficial insects.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Adaptable soil types
  • Self-seeds readily

Cultivars

  • ‘Prairie Glow’

Symbolism

Represents abundance, resilience, and natural wild beauty.
