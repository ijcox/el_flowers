Rudbeckia laciniata – Cutleaf Coneflower

Black-Eyed Susan laciniata is a tall, vigorous perennial known for its deeply divided leaves and bright yellow flowers.

Description

Produces large yellow daisy-like flowers with greenish central cones on tall, branching stems with deeply lobed foliage.

Etymology

“Laciniata” means “torn” or “deeply cut,” referring to the shape of its leaves.

Taxonomy

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Rudbeckia
Species: R. laciniata

Distribution and Habitat

Native to North America, commonly found in moist meadows, riverbanks, and forest edges.

Ecology

Supports pollinators such as bees and butterflies; seeds are eaten by birds in late season.

Cultivation

  • Full sun to partial shade
  • Moist, fertile soil
  • Can spread aggressively in ideal conditions

Cultivars

  • ‘Hortensia’ (double-flowered form)

Symbolism

Represents growth, vitality, and untamed natural energy.
