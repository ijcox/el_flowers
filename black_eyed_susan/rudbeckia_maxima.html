Rudbeckia maxima – Giant Coneflower

Black-Eyed Susan maxima is a tall, dramatic perennial known for its towering flower spikes and blue-green foliage.

Description

Produces large yellow daisy-like flowers with prominent dark cones atop very tall stems and striking waxy leaves.

Etymology

“Maxima” means “largest,” referring to its impressive height and flower size.

Taxonomy

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Rudbeckia
Species: R. maxima

Distribution and Habitat

Native to the southern United States, especially Texas and surrounding regions, in prairies and moist lowlands.

Ecology

Attracts bees, butterflies, and birds; seed heads are often eaten by finches.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Moist, well-drained soil
  • Needs space due to tall growth

Cultivars

  • Mainly wild-type in cultivation

Symbolism

Represents strength, elevation, and natural grandeur.
