Chrysanthemums

Chrysanthemums are highly diverse flowering plants prized for their richly layered blooms and long history of cultivation. They are among the most important ornamental flowers in the world, especially in Asia and Europe.

Description

Chrysanthemums vary widely in form, from simple daisy-like flowers to densely petaled pompons and decorative sprays. Leaves are typically lobed or toothed and aromatic when crushed.

Flower colors include white, yellow, pink, red, purple, and bronze. Bloom times vary by cultivar, but many flower in late summer through autumn.

Taxonomy

Chrysanthemums belong to the genus Chrysanthemum in the family Asteraceae. Many cultivated varieties were once placed in related genera such as Dendranthema.

Species

Notable species include:

Chrysanthemum Morifolium – Florist’s chrysanthemum
Chrysanthemum Indicum – Indian chrysanthemum
Chrysanthemum Zawadskii – Siberian species
Chrysanthemum Arcticum – Arctic chrysanthemum
Chrysanthemum Nankingense – Wild ancestor species

Etymology

The name comes from Greek chrysos meaning “gold” and anthemon meaning “flower,” referring to early yellow-flowered forms.

Ecology

Chrysanthemums are important late-season nectar sources for pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Wild species occur in East Asia and parts of Europe, often in grasslands and rocky slopes.

Some species are adapted to cooler climates and can tolerate light frosts.

Diseases and pests

  • Aphids commonly infest buds and shoots.
  • Spider mites may appear in dry indoor conditions.
  • Powdery mildew can develop in crowded plantings.
  • Leaf miners may cause visible trails in leaves.
  • Rust fungi can affect foliage in humid environments.

Cultivation

Chrysanthemums are widely grown for gardens, cut flowers, and potted displays. They are often used in seasonal floral arrangements and festivals.

  • Light: Full sun for strong flowering.
  • Soil: Well-drained, fertile soil.
  • Water: Regular moisture without waterlogging.
  • Pinching: Encourages bushier growth and more blooms.
  • Propagation: Cuttings or division.

Chrysanthemums in culture

Chrysanthemums symbolize longevity, joy, and nobility in many Asian cultures. In Japan they are associated with the Imperial family and celebrated in national festivals.

In Europe, they are often used in autumn displays and remembrance traditions.
