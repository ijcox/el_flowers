Gerbera jamesonii – Barberton Daisy

Daisy jamesonii is a vibrant flowering species widely cultivated for its large, colorful blooms used in floral arrangements.

Description

A perennial herb producing large daisy-like flowers in bright shades of red, orange, yellow, pink, and white on long stems.

Etymology

Named after Robert Jameson, who helped document the species in South Africa.

Taxonomy

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Gerbera
Species: G. jamesonii

Distribution and Habitat

Native to South Africa, especially the Barberton region, growing in grasslands and rocky slopes.

Ecology

Attracts bees and other pollinators and is an important ornamental crop worldwide.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Moderate watering
  • Common in greenhouse and garden cultivation

Cultivars

  • ‘Festival’ series – bright mixed colors
  • ‘Garvinea’ series – garden hardy types

Symbolism

Represents cheerfulness, innocence, and beauty.
