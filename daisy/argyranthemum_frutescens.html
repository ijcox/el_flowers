Argyranthemum frutescens – Marguerite Daisy

Daisy frutescens is a shrubby daisy species widely grown for its abundant long-lasting blooms.

Description

A woody perennial shrub producing masses of daisy-like flowers in white, pink, or yellow with finely divided foliage.

Etymology

“Argyranthemum” means “silver flower,” referring to the pale appearance of some foliage and blooms.

Taxonomy

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Argyranthemum
Species: A. frutescens

Distribution and Habitat

Native to the Canary Islands, commonly cultivated in Mediterranean and temperate gardens.

Ecology

Attracts bees and butterflies and thrives in coastal and sunny environments.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Regular deadheading encourages flowering
  • Frost tender in cold climates

Cultivars

  • ‘Madeira Cream’ – pale yellow flowers
  • ‘Butterfly’ series – compact decorative forms

Symbolism

Represents cheerfulness, warmth, and garden abundance.
