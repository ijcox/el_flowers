Leucanthemum × superbum – Shasta Daisy

Daisy × superbum is a hybrid daisy known for its large, bright white flowers and strong garden performance.

Description

A perennial plant producing large white petals surrounding a golden-yellow central disc, held on sturdy upright stems.

Etymology

“Superbum” refers to its superior ornamental qualities compared to wild daisy species.

Taxonomy

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Leucanthemum
Species: L. × superbum

Distribution and Habitat

A cultivated hybrid developed in Europe and North America, widely grown in gardens worldwide.

Ecology

Attracts bees and butterflies and is valued in pollinator-friendly gardens.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Deadheading encourages prolonged blooming
  • Cold hardy perennial

Cultivars

  • ‘Alaska’ – classic large white blooms
  • ‘Becky’ – strong, tall garden form

Symbolism

Represents purity, optimism, and simplicity.
