Bellis perennis – Common Daisy

Daisy perennis is a small, cheerful perennial flower commonly found in lawns and meadows across temperate regions.

Description

A low-growing perennial forming rosettes of spoon-shaped leaves and producing white petals surrounding a yellow central disc.

Etymology

“Bellis” means “pretty,” and “perennis” means “perennial,” referring to its long-lasting, attractive nature.

Taxonomy

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Bellis
Species: B. perennis

Distribution and Habitat

Native to Europe and Western Asia, now naturalized worldwide in lawns, grasslands, and disturbed areas.

Ecology

Provides nectar and pollen for bees and small insects, especially in early spring and summer.

Cultivation

  • Full sun to partial shade
  • Adaptable to many soil types
  • Often grows naturally in lawns
  • Low maintenance perennial

Cultivars

  • ‘Pomponette’ – double-flowered form
  • ‘Tasso’ series – compact ornamental varieties

Symbolism

Represents innocence, simplicity, and new beginnings.
