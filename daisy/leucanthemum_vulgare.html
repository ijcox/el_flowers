Leucanthemum vulgare – Oxeye Daisy

Daisy vulgare is a widespread wildflower known for its classic white petals and yellow central disc.

Description

A perennial herb with upright stems bearing single white flower heads with a bright yellow center.

Etymology

“Leucanthemum” means “white flower,” and “vulgare” means “common.”

Taxonomy

Family: Asteraceae
Genus: Leucanthemum
Species: L. vulgare

Distribution and Habitat

Native to Europe and Asia, now naturalized in North America and many other regions.

Ecology

Supports pollinators but can become invasive in some grassland ecosystems.

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Very hardy perennial
  • Spreads easily in open areas

Cultivars

  • ‘May Queen’ – early blooming form

Symbolism

Represents simplicity, purity, and resilience.
