Rhododendron

Rhododendrons are woody flowering shrubs and trees known for their large clusters of showy blooms and evergreen foliage.

Description

Rhododendron flowers grow in rounded clusters called trusses and appear in shades of pink, purple, red, white, and yellow. Leaves are often thick, leathery, and evergreen.

They are widely used in ornamental landscaping and woodland gardens.

Taxonomy

Rhododendron belongs to the genus Rhododendron in the family Ericaceae. The genus includes both rhododendrons and azaleas.

Species

Rhododendron Ponticum – Common Rhododendron
Rhododendron Catawbiense – Catawba Rhododendron
Rhododendron Ferrugineum – Alpine Rose
Rhododendron Arboreum – Tree Rhododendron

Etymology

The name Rhododendron comes from Greek meaning “rose tree.”

Ecology

Rhododendrons thrive in acidic soils and provide shelter and nectar for pollinators in woodland ecosystems.

Diseases and pests

  • Root rot in poorly drained soil
  • Leaf spot fungal diseases
  • Weevils feeding on leaves
  • Aphids on young shoots

Cultivation

Rhododendrons are popular ornamental shrubs in temperate and woodland gardens.

  • Light: Partial shade
  • Soil: Acidic, well-drained soil
  • Water: Regular moisture
  • Planting: Nursery-grown shrubs
  • Maintenance: Mulch to retain moisture

Rhododendron in culture

Rhododendrons symbolize elegance, caution, and beauty, and are celebrated in many botanical gardens worldwide.
