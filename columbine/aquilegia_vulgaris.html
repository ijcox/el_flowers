Aquilegia vulgaris – European Columbine

Columbine vulgaris is a traditional European garden perennial known for its elegant, spurred flowers and cottage-garden charm.

Description

A herbaceous perennial producing nodding flowers with distinctive backward-pointing spurs, typically in shades of purple, blue, pink, or white.

Etymology

“Aquilegia” comes from Latin meaning “eagle-like,” referring to the shape of its spurred petals resembling eagle claws.

Taxonomy

Family: Ranunculaceae
Genus: Aquilegia
Species: A. vulgaris

Distribution and Habitat

Native to Europe, commonly found in woodland edges, meadows, and shaded slopes, and widely cultivated in gardens.

Ecology

Pollinated mainly by bees, especially long-tongued species adapted to reach the nectar in its spurred flowers.

Cultivation

  • Partial shade to full sun
  • Moist, well-drained soil
  • Self-seeds readily in gardens
  • Short-lived perennial but persists through reseeding

Cultivars

  • ‘Nora Barlow’ – double pink-green flowers
  • ‘Black Barlow’ – deep maroon double blooms

Symbolism

Represents wisdom, faith, and the complexity of nature.
