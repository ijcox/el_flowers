Aquilegia alpina – Alpine Columbine

Columbine alpina is a striking high-altitude species native to alpine regions, valued for its large blue flowers.

Description

A compact perennial producing large, deep blue to violet flowers with prominent spurs, adapted to cold mountain environments.

Etymology

“Alpina” refers to its natural occurrence in alpine mountain habitats.

Taxonomy

Family: Ranunculaceae
Genus: Aquilegia
Species: A. alpina

Distribution and Habitat

Native to the European Alps and mountainous regions, growing in rocky slopes and alpine meadows.

Ecology

Adapted to short growing seasons and pollinated by alpine insects such as bumblebees.

Cultivation

  • Full sun to partial shade
  • Cool, well-drained soil
  • Best suited for rock gardens
  • Requires good drainage in winter

Cultivars

  • Primarily wild species in cultivation

Symbolism

Represents endurance, purity, and resilience in harsh environments.
