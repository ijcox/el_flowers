Aquilegia chrysantha – Golden Columbine

Columbine chrysantha is a bright yellow-flowered species known for its long spurs and attraction to pollinators in dry regions.

Description

A tall perennial producing luminous golden-yellow flowers with elongated spurs and delicate foliage.

Etymology

“Chrysantha” means “golden flower,” referring to its bright yellow blooms.

Taxonomy

Family: Ranunculaceae
Genus: Aquilegia
Species: A. chrysantha

Distribution and Habitat

Native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, found in canyon walls and streamside habitats.

Ecology

Highly attractive to hummingbirds and long-tongued insects due to its nectar-rich spurs.

Cultivation

  • Full sun to light shade
  • Well-drained soil
  • Drought tolerant once established
  • Ideal for xeriscaping gardens

Cultivars

  • ‘Yellow Queen’ – improved ornamental selection

Symbolism

Represents brightness, vitality, and natural brilliance.
