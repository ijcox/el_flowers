Aquilegia caerulea – Colorado Columbine

Columbine caerulea is the state flower of Colorado, admired for its striking blue, white, and yellow blossoms.

Description

A graceful perennial producing large flowers with blue sepals, white petals, and yellow stamens, often considered one of the most beautiful columbines.

Etymology

“Caerulea” means “sky blue,” referring to the characteristic coloration of its sepals.

Taxonomy

Family: Ranunculaceae
Genus: Aquilegia
Species: A. caerulea

Distribution and Habitat

Native to the Rocky Mountains of North America, growing in alpine meadows and forest clearings.

Ecology

Important nectar source for hummingbirds and bees in high-elevation ecosystems.

Cultivation

  • Partial shade preferred in warmer climates
  • Cool, well-drained soil
  • Suitable for alpine and woodland gardens
  • Moderate watering needs

Cultivars

  • ‘Kirigami’ series – uniform, compact hybrids
  • Selected blue-white ornamental strains

Symbolism

Represents endurance, natural beauty, and high mountain resilience.
