Aquilegia canadensis – Red Columbine

Columbine canadensis is a vibrant North American wildflower known for its red and yellow nodding blooms that attract hummingbirds.

Description

A perennial wildflower producing red outer sepals with yellow inner petals and long spurs adapted for pollinators with long beaks.

Etymology

“Canadensis” means “from Canada,” referring to its native North American range.

Taxonomy

Family: Ranunculaceae
Genus: Aquilegia
Species: A. canadensis

Distribution and Habitat

Native to eastern North America, found in woodlands, rocky slopes, and open forests.

Ecology

Specially adapted for hummingbird pollination due to its tubular red flowers and nectar production.

Cultivation

  • Partial shade to full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Drought tolerant once established
  • Attracts pollinators and wildlife

Cultivars

  • Natural wild form most commonly grown

Symbolism

Represents endurance, energy, and attraction of life and movement.
