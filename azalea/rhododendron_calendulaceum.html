Rhododendron calendulaceum – Flame Azalea

Azalea calendulaceum is a spectacular deciduous shrub known for its intense flame-colored blooms.

Description

Produces vivid orange, red, and yellow funnel-shaped flowers that appear before or with leaf emergence in late spring.

Etymology

“Calendulaceum” refers to its marigold-like (Calendula) flower coloration.

Taxonomy

Family: Ericaceae
Genus: Rhododendron
Species: R. calendulaceum

Distribution and Habitat

Native to the Appalachian Mountains in the United States, found in open woodlands and mountain slopes.

Ecology

Attracts hummingbirds, bees, and butterflies; thrives in acidic forest soils.

Cultivation

  • Full sun to partial shade
  • Acidic, well-drained soil
  • Consistent moisture without waterlogging

Cultivars

  • ‘Golden Sunset’ selections
  • Wild-type breeding forms

Symbolism

Represents passion, energy, and the fiery beauty of mountain landscapes.
