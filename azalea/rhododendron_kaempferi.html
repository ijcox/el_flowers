Rhododendron kaempferi – Torch Azalea

Azalea kaempferi is a hardy deciduous shrub known for its bright flame-colored blossoms and strong ornamental presence.

Description

Medium-sized shrub producing vivid red, orange, or salmon-colored funnel-shaped flowers in spring before full leaf-out.

Etymology

Named after Engelbert Kaempfer, a German botanist who studied Japanese flora in the 17th century.

Taxonomy

Family: Ericaceae
Genus: Rhododendron
Species: R. kaempferi

Distribution and Habitat

Native to Japan, especially mountainous forest regions and slopes with acidic soils.

Ecology

Attracts bees and early-season pollinators; adapted to cool, moist environments.

Cultivation

  • Partial sun to light shade
  • Acidic, well-drained soil
  • Moderate watering

Cultivars

  • ‘Crimson Form’
  • ‘Johanna’

Symbolism

Represents passion, vitality, and the arrival of spring energy.
