Rhododendron indicum – Satsuki Azalea

Azalea indicum (Satsuki azalea) is a compact evergreen shrub prized in Japanese horticulture for its late spring to early summer blooms.

Description

Low-growing evergreen shrub with small glossy leaves and abundant funnel-shaped flowers in pink, red, white, and bi-color patterns.

Etymology

“Rhododendron” comes from Greek meaning “rose tree,” while “indicum” refers to its historical association with India and East Asia trade routes.

Taxonomy

Family: Ericaceae
Genus: Rhododendron
Species: R. indicum

Distribution and Habitat

Native to Japan and cultivated widely in East Asia. Found in forest edges and rocky slopes.

Ecology

Pollinated by bees and butterflies; prefers acidic soils and sheltered environments.

Cultivation

  • Partial shade
  • Acidic, well-drained soil
  • Regular pruning for shaping (bonsai use common)

Cultivars

  • ‘Osakazuki’
  • ‘Shinnyo-no-tsuki’

Symbolism

Represents harmony, patience, and refined beauty in Japanese garden culture.
