Rhododendron viscosum – Swamp Azalea

Azalea viscosum is a fragrant deciduous shrub native to wetland habitats of eastern North America.

Description

Produces white to pale pink tubular flowers with a strong sweet fragrance in early to midsummer. Leaves are narrow and glossy.

Etymology

“Viscosum” refers to the sticky or glandular nature of young shoots and flower parts.

Taxonomy

Family: Ericaceae
Genus: Rhododendron
Species: R. viscosum

Distribution and Habitat

Native to eastern North America, commonly found in swamps, bogs, and wet woodland edges.

Ecology

Highly attractive to pollinators including bees, moths, and butterflies due to its strong fragrance.

Cultivation

  • Partial sun to full sun
  • Moist, acidic, organic-rich soil
  • Consistent water availability required

Cultivars

  • ‘Clarke’s Sweet Azalea’ selections

Symbolism

Represents resilience, purity, and natural wetland beauty.
