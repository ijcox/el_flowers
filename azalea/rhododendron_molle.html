Rhododendron molle – Mollis Azalea

Azalea molle is a deciduous azalea known for its large, soft-colored flowers and early spring display.

Description

Medium shrub producing broad funnel-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and soft red before or alongside leaf emergence.

Etymology

“Molle” means soft or tender in Latin, referring to its soft foliage and gentle flower tones.

Taxonomy

Family: Ericaceae
Genus: Rhododendron
Species: R. molle

Distribution and Habitat

Native to China, growing in woodland margins and mountainous regions with acidic soils.

Ecology

Attracts bees and butterflies; adapted to seasonal climates with cool winters.

Cultivation

  • Full sun to partial shade
  • Acidic, well-drained soil
  • Mulching recommended

Cultivars

  • ‘Golden Lights’ hybrids
  • ‘Narcissiflora’ group forms

Symbolism

Represents softness, renewal, and early spring awakening.
